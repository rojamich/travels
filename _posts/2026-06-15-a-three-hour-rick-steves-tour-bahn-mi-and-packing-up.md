There was a photoshoot going on at the breakfast spot so we were provided entertainment to go along with our meal XD

We set out west to the beginning of our three-hour Rick Steve's audio tour of "Historic London: The City" - the name of this district within London!

We began sitting on a shady bench in a bustling square between a church that is actually a memorial to the service members of WWII and the building used for the Gringotts Wizarding Bank in Harry Potter - WHAT! What a nice surprise!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781660945/IMG_9800x_lwj2ba.jpg)

The tour took us on a long, eastbound walk and our second destination was the Royal Courts of Justice - how is this a court house?!

We stood in awe of this intricately crafted castle-looking building. It looks like Hogwarts to me! Why are we using this for court cases instead of weddings or massive movie backdrops?!

Famous court cases that took place here were: Spice Girls suing the tabloids, Dan Brown (author of the Da Vinci Code) sued for plagiarism, and the Beatles members divorce settlements. 

We noticed that there weren't many tea establishments or advertisements, apparently coffee is on a huge comeback in London and we can't go a block without one (to my everlasting happiness). 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781660702/IMG_9807_mcx4yx.jpg)
