You may not know that when I met Mike (almost exactly 13 years ago!) I had zero interest in travel. He originally talked about places he would like to see in his life and I said I had no motivation to ever leave Sacramento. It was an easy comfort zone and what more could the world have to offer than California?

He was so patient with my naïveté and essentially baby-stepped my cautious self all the way here, to Africa. I still wake up and cannot believe we are really here. Especially after counting down the months for the past year!

Side note, for those curious for future travel, he is using Airalo for simcard usage. You can choose which country and pick a data plan for the time you spend in each country. It worked moderately well in Costa Rica and so far it's worked well in South Africa.

After sleeping so much over the flights, we only managed to sleep until 3am this day. Excitement probably played a role in this as well! I headed to the gym while Mike worked on his languages.

We stopped by the hotel breakfast, not expecting much, only to be met with an absolute smorgasbord!

It had more than I could even name. You could special order your eggs how you like them with all the fixings (I tried macon for the first time which is mutton bacon, it was good!)

We were picked up by our amazing tour guide for a full 11 hour tour, exploring the best of South Africa! Our original plan was to rent a car and drive to each of these stops. However, we weren't sure about how well driving here would go and this tour hit literally every highight we wanted to see, win win! We loaded up the van and headed to our first stop, Bo-Kaap, we actually live in this area! Check out the brightly colored homes! This is not regulated and is chosen by the residents! The owners painted them as an expression of freedom after the ban was lifted that required them all to be white. Originally, any nonwhite residents were unable to own property and they had to rent. The owners at that time required each house to be white.

Including this gentleman who jokingly jumped into our photo, he is a tour guide for another group! Everyone seems to know each other here in the city of nearly 6 million people. People are so friendly and readily help others here.
