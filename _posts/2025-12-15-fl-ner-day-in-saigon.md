That's right! Many people still refer to Ho Chi Minh City (HCMC) as Saigon, despite the name being changed on July 2, 1976, after the Vietnam War ended. We woke up early on our first day in Vietnam and needed local currency (Vietnamese Dong) to purchase breakfast. Many places (especially authentic food) will be cash only. 

Stepping outside our hotel was an instant sensory overload. The road was packed with cars, mopeds, and motorbikes. Neon signs adorned every building. There were cars honking and sidewalks were filled with people, food carts, and mopeds (yes, they were on the sidewalk sometimes!). The delicious smells from different types of foods permeated the air. We felt like we couldn't take it all in fast enough. 

We were getting pretty hangry, so we thought it best to grab an easy breakfast from a convenience store nearby. We loved the convenience stores in Japan and Korea! Vietnam also has good food options in theirs!

The portion sizes are quite different here, haha. The actual chip sizes are tinier, too!

It was finally time to try the famously strong Vietnamese coffee. To my utter astonishment, they actually offer sugary flavors at their coffee shops! In other countries, I was met with disgust when I asked if there were flavored options. I gave the cara-melting latte a try! Mike had the iced coffee straight, like a crazy person. We were able to order in Vietnamese! Mike and I have been taking lessons from a Vietnamese tutor online and practicing using the Mango Languages app and flashcards to prepare for the trip :) It has been very hard but fun! Vietnamese is a tonal language, meaning the inflection of your voice changes the meaning of the word. An example, there are six different ways to say La, and they are all different words. Crazy right?!

We chose the 2 hands, 100 minutes option, and we never even had to leave the bed! They got right to work cleaning our ears while we soaked our feet. The ear cleaning felt odd but nice! The massage was deep tissue, and I think I forgot my name at one point. I was so relaxed! My favorite thing about here so far is the intense hair washing. Do you know how long it takes and what a big pain it is to wash and dry my hair?? I have SO MUCH HAIR!! It was nice to have that chore taken care of. The VIP package of massages cost us $70 USD total. At home, it's roughly $200 total for Mike and me just to get a massage.

  


We were positively famished afterwards and saw a Phở restaurant that was filled with locals. You bet that's the spot for us! We were seated at the only free table, and I ordered the Phở Bà (beef pho) while Mike tried the Phở Gà (chicken pho).

There were a ton of options for spices and sauces at the table to apply to the Phở, but we tried it without first. It was flavorful, but I like spice! So I started trying random items at the table, including those little green chilis. They're called  Thai bird's eye chilis and, let me tell you, they are not the normal kind of chilis we have at home. Holy. Sh*t. That's hot!! But... they looked like the ones at home. So, naturally, I had dumped about 6 of them into my soup. I found out too late. It was a hot soup. I tried asking the staff what kind of chilis they were - they just said green chilis and became concerned that I was unhappy. Thankfully, I had asked our tutor how to say, "I like spicy food!" in Vietnamese, so they were happy that I was happy. We each had a _**very**_ filling meal for $4 USD total!

  


Two doors down from that was a recommended nail place! I made a pretty big decision (well, big for me) to try fake nails. I've never done it before, and I've never felt like I could when I was in law enforcement, when in Vietnam, right?!

  


I found a design online I thought would look cool, while Mike and I had our feet soaking for pedicures, a manicurist got to work on my nails! Holy moly, it's hot when they stick them to your fingers! Mike spoke Vietnamese to his pedicurist, and she was IN SHOCK! She said, "Why do you speak Vietnamese? Did you move here?" Mike explained, "No, we are just visiting." She loved that, and we were proud 😊
