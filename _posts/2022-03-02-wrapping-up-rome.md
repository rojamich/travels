We got back to the room with enough time to finish planning Germany and pack most of our things for the trip to Venice the next day. Mike found us an Italian escape room across town, the only thing I was nervous about was…taking the bus! And I was right to be nervous! The bus line we wanted never came. We waited and waited until we were forced to try another one. Everyone pushed onto the bus until we were packed like sardines in a can. This would be a fun thirty minute ride… At least no one touched anything they shouldn’t or even smelled! The downside was I was starting to get pretty nauseous. The bus was gentrified and the ride was miserable.

When we arrived at the escape room I was in a pretty bad mood. I am grateful we had a good gamemaster who was trying his hardest and begged us to pardon his rusty English. It was a cute room with a cool theme but the puzzles didn’t make sense to the story. Overall, I was just not a happy goose. Mike got into it and was great about trying to improve the night for me. Love that man. We had to crawl through a tunnel inside, Mike got his magnetic clues, there was treasure and Indiana Jones music. The theme was Indiana Jones and the Treasure of Rome. We were there for an hour and forty minutes!!!

The décor of the actual rooms was beautiful though!

The women who worked at the shop were so sweet and funny. She was singing and dancing to the music so I did too! With one gelato in each hand!
