We enjoyed our last beautiful breakfast in Sankt Goar and took no bite for granted before we continued onto Nuremburg. We knew the breakfasts would not be as fulfilling so we savored every moment. It was pretty chilly this day as we jogged down the hill (it was so steep there was no other way to get down it) to get to our train. We had to ride North just to connect to a train that was going South lol. 

Unfortunately, our first train was delayed which meant our second train would be missed by 25 minutes! We had booked the cheapest ticket option which meant the train company did not have to reimburse or reschedule us if our delay was under 30 minutes. We went to the information booth when we got to the larger train station and the guy was the kindest man! He said 25 minutes was close enough and even booked us tickets for a straight through train ride to Nuremburg! We would’ve had two more connections otherwise! What a win! Go Mike go! The ride was pleasant and we arrived in Nuremburg about 1330. I thought this sign was pretty cool welcoming visitors in many languages!

One thing to note is that, like much everywhere else we visited in Europe, everyone and their literal mothers smoked here. It was everywhere (thankfully not inside) but many places smelt of it anyways. Young, old, men, women, it didn’t matter. Everyone smokes.

As we self-toured the city, we ended up knocking some of the wander-log sights off our list. Our time in Nuremburg was short and we had to take advantage of it! We started with this odd fountain that is a sculpture-form of the poem called “Bitter-sweet Married Life”. It depicts the painful aspects that sometimes ruin marriages (gluttony, lust, envy, etc).
