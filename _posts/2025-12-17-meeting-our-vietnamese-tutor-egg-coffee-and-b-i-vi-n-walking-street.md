That's right! Our tutor (Thu Hằng) lives in Ho Chi Minh City! I met her through my manicurist (May) back in Corvallis! When Mike and I started learning Vietnamese, I overheard staff at P&P nails and asked if they could correct me if I said a few words. They were surprised and ecstatic :) They gave me tons of advice and recommendations! May told me that her daughter was taking lessons in Vietnamese and offered to connect us with the same tutor. We met with Thu Hằng over Zoom for the next 5 weeks and learned a lot! She asked to meet when we visited, and we were only too happy to make that happen 😁

After a quick morning workout, we headed to another visit to Cơm tôm. It was delicious once more! The owner told us, "Day off Saturday. Day off Sunday!" Which, as a creature of habit, I appreciated knowing ahead of time that it wouldn't be available for breakfast the next two days, haha! Thankfully, laundry was free at the hotel, so we got that started while we watched the football game online (TNF in the US is Friday morning football in Vietnam). Did you know we are about 9 hours ahead of PST time?

Have we talked about crossing the roads here in Vietnam? No? Ok, let me shed some light on that situation. Cars don't stop. Period. You go around the cars. There are roughly 77 million motorbikes in Vietnam, with 8.5 million of them in HCMC alone. The traffic here is downright chaotic. Even if there is a traffic light signaling you to walk, you may still have to dodge traffic to cross the road. You just have to have your head on a constant swivel, dodge the cars, and walk with confidence and eye contact with motorbikes to ensure who is going around whom. 

That being said, our tutor, Thu Hằng, was very apprehensive when we were preparing to cross the road. Maybe we have been going a little too confidently?? So, when we crossed the first time, we noticed she was barely inching across the road. We raced back into the intersection, and she grabbed my hand like I was pulling her to shore.   


When we went to cross the second time, she decided she couldn't lose me if we held hands lol. She is absolutely adorable, and I would protect her with my life. I had to bend down to hear her quiet voice!

Each restaurant/club was BLARING their techno music, and scantily-clad males and females danced on tables or stood in the street to lure you inside. There were throngs of hosts/hostesses in the middle of the street, ushering people into their establishments. I'm not sure how they could work with the noise for that long!

The dancers were surgically modified with Botox and/or implants and had zero facial expressions. We were certain they were NPCs! An NPC is a non-player character in video games that is not controlled by a human but is pre-programmed into the game.
