I woke up with a bittersweet feeling filling my heart. You may be thinking "boo hoo, she has to come home from vacation" and I wouldn't blame you. But I've loved adventuring with Mike and breaking out of our comfort zones, and learning and laughing together. 

But I am excited to see my family and friends again,  to eat consistent, healthy meals, have a regular workout schedule, and sleep in my comfy bed. But I am nervous about my next chapter too! It's a bit of stress to jump back into but it will be great too. 

Anyway! Our bags were packed and breakfast pizza was consumed,  we walked down the hill for the last time to the local coffee/pastry shop. We sat and relaxed and enjoyed our pastry and Mike had his espresso shot. 

We sat by Lake Como one last time and enjoyed the serenity.

We hit our favorite gelato/cannoli shop once more and got coffee for the train. This train was PACKED and Mike somehow worked his magic to get us seats and we enjoyed another 40-minute train ride.

We were early for our flight and there weren't any food options (unless you count a cafe with prepackaged sandwiches, which I do not)  so we boarded before dinner. Naturally, our plane was delayed for an hour and a half with us sitting on the tarmac. We passed a teen crying, surrounded by family, as we boarded and found out during boarding that she was having a panic attack on the plane and kept demanding to be let off. Her mother was irate. 

So, we all sat on the tarmac while her mother, a doctor, and the flight attendants tried to calm her down. No luck, after and hour and a half, she and her mother deboarded and we were able to take off. It was sad to hear the disparaging comments made by other passengers who are less understanding when it comes to mental health. 

However, we were later rewarded with a beautiful sunset as we flew two hours to London.
