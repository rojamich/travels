Heading out for the day, we learned that driving here can initially be very stressful! There is a lot of traffic at times and the signs are not always clear. Thankfully we haven't gone the wrong way down any one-ways or parked somewhere we shouldn't! Mike was very excited to drive on the Autobahn though as they have no speedlimits in many places! So, without traffic, we are often going 115mph!

There is also something called the "Rettungsgasse" here which is their version of an emergency lane. The shoulders on the road are for broken down cars. So when the traffic slows, the law is that the very left lane gets as far to the left as they can and the second to left lane gets as far to the right as they can. This creates a middle space for any incoming emergency traffic.

Other times there is odd structuring where it goes from 120km to 80 to 100 in the span of 50m! We miss the trains but the driving has made us more flexible and less rushed. 

  


After a long drive, we made it into Stuttgart and began exploring the town. We started by spotting the Stiftskirche (a historic church with stained glass windows) and admired it as we strolled to the marketplace.

After, we found the Marienplatz which had a massive, live jazz event! We also walked the Schillerplatz which was filled with cobblestone and a statue. It was really neat to admire the architecture!

  


It was time to try a doner! Mike was eagerly waiting to try this so we can compare it to Gyros in Greece. Parking near here was really challenging because the parking meters weren't working for us! So we took turns running up to the restaurant while the other parked, seeing what we, wanted, switching, switching back to use the bathroom, so on and so forth. Eventually we got our food and headed towards a park that overlooked the city.

We found a bench and ate our doners and admired the view in this huge city park!
