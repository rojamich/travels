Being the romantic man that he is, we walked over to the Lock Bridge that I was so longing to go to after seeing Leslie Knope go there in an episode of Parks & Recreation. Unfortunately, due to the bridge’s construction restraints, no locks are allowed to be attached to the bridge. They have all been removed and plexiglass placed over the bridge parts. So, we found a loop hole. Ours is the bright gold one nearest me. We attached it, took a photo, and brought it back with us to hang as an ornament on our Christmas tree.

The Pantheon is a come-when-you-want museum and stunning to see! We got in and the employee forgot to charge us for two tickets so she gave us a free one! How cool! It was the coldest museum I have ever been in (go figure a museum of stone is cold).

Mike describes it as mixture of a museum and church (That is also what it has been throughout history lol). The solemnness of a church with the history of a museum. I accidentally took a photo of it in black and white but I think it looks neat!

We made it to the Père Lachaise Cemetery which houses Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, and other famous deceased. We had no idea this was still an active cemetery and there was actually a funeral going on at the time. The tombstones were massive! Some looked like the size of studio apartments in San Diego.

We started running out of time for our last day! We had to hurry. We dropped off the laundry at the laundromat near the hotel and I took my COVID test first to enter Italy. Mike switched the laundry and I received a negative result (whew!) Michael then took his test while I finished the laundry. He was also negative! (Double whew!) We arrived at our final escape room in the nick of time! 

This one was Clue themed! One of my favorite board games! Our game master was nerdy, adorable, and into the game! We really enjoyed her and had a phenomenal time. Another great room and this required good deduction methods!

After the room we packed up and prepared for our journey to Italy early the next morning. We will really miss the pastries, escape rooms and stellar hotel suite. Only a few days before departure, we were getting pretty worried about being able to enter Italy, solely for travel purposes. Italy had been one of the more strict countries regarding COVID due to the large elderly population. Thankfully, Mike discovered just a few days before leaving, that we would _likely_ be allowed in the country!
