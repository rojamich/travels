We (surprisingly) quickly adapted to the lifestyle in Georgia and woke up around 10am this morning! I thought we would be done with eye covers now that we left the summer sun area of Norway but this new lifestyle makes them necessary! 

We made a quick breakfast and set up house - where will I put this? And that? What do we need? Let's make a grocery list!

A few hours later we set out for the bazaar and decided to use the local transport. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784534681/20260715_123554_ax9dyh.jpg)

If you didn't know, there are tens of thousands of stray dogs here! They are tagged, vaccinated, and spayed/neutered and monitored by the government. The ones in the cities are generally safe and docile but in rural areas can be a mix. I want them all 😥\
\
We took the tunnel underground to cross the street and this bakery has smelled *so* good each time we have passed it. We asked the woman what the most popular choices were and she said the apple pie and the baklava. We opted to try the "apple pie" 🥧 and it was solid! It was not like our pie but it was very sugary and good. **Super** heavy too!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784534721/20260715_133519_vnmzmv.jpg "Us trying out the local Georgian bus!")

The  Deserter bazaar was about an hour walk so we thought it would be cool to use the bus system here - its so efficient and there are tons of buses running constantly! It cost us $1.14 for us both to get a 20 minute ride across town 😲

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1784535337%2F20260715_135825_wfsjem.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1784535339%2F20260715_135829_xuqdin.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1784535339%2F20260715_135827_bfdtpg.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

Wow, this is overwhelming! The streets are lined with stalls and umbrellas to shade those in chairs with batches of fruit and wares. There's no way to take in everything at once. Electronics, clothes, jerseys, bags, and shoes - ***SO*** many shoes!! There's another section for ingredients too! Needless to say, it was a chaotic and toasty experience. The bartering is minimal and seems cranky. I need to keep in mind they aren't mad, just unsmiling here - a cultural norm!

I start to get hot and hungry so we look for a lunch spot nearby and come up with a tasty looking gyro spot. 

Mike uses my phone to look this up while I use the restroom and realizes that my phone background can change each time I open it if you put it on slideshow mode. So these are the different faces I see every time I open my phone 🤣😍

10 minutes later we stop in front of an empty store front and realize that is normal here - many locations say open but no longer exist or are closed 😫 I'm so hungry and tired!\
We search for another spot and settle on the insanely fancy, two-story McDonald's nearby (as per our tradition)! Something super interesting we learned was that "McDonald's in Tbilisi offers a healthier, higher-quality taste than the US due to stricter EU food regulations." This is what made our food taste way fresher and better than usual for McDonalds 🤯

Now we are on a mission to see the large grocery store in Tbilisi called "Carrefour Market" - we search for the bus and head out! Waiting at the bus stop, we see many buses with 300 numbers and some with 400 and 500s. Hm, I wonder what the differences are? Some are labeled green and others blue. Ours is blue and 500 level. We quickly find out these are, in fact, marshrutka minivans!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1784537192%2F20260715_162937_eto5uo.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1784537191%2F20260715_162926_bnjd43.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

We squeeze in, in the back and are transported with locals around Tbilisi - what a freaking adventure today! The van is very toasty and Mike is not a fan. (literally but he also is not enjoying this van haha). We have to look up how to flag the driver for a stop and were hoping someone else would get off first. Unfortunately, everyone seems to be here for the long haul so Mike bravely stands up and says some Georgian words we have never uttered before. At first, there is some confusion but they quickly realize we are asking to stop and we are dropped at the market bus stop. 

Holy crap what a gorgeous building!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784537872/20260715_163902_vhyfrz.jpg)

We pass a huge flower market and peek into the grocery store that spans a city block! Next door, there's more shops and a ton of food stalls!

This is where we found the vitamin shop and got our protein powder so we can ensure we hit our protein goals while we are here. Plus it makes for an easy daily smoothie meal!

Back at the grocery store, Mike grabbed a basket which resembles a laundry basket back home 🧺

We got a blender and french press to make our favorites! Thankfully, they were reasonably priced and (spoiler, work very well!)

We saw Georgian candy (its everywhere here!) called Georgian snickers because it's chewy and nutty!
