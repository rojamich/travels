While we enjoyed Osaka the previous day, we debated what our last day in Japan would hold. Every evening, we reviewed the next day's itinerary, got excited for things to come, and planned. We concluded that our return to Japan in the future would be entirely too long from now to miss a trip to Hiroshima.

Ideally, we would've taken three months to explore Japan fully, including the northern, central, southern, and outlying islands. We didn't have enough time on this trip (school is out for a month for winter break) and I'm not ready for the humidity of Asia in the summer! With all of this in mind, we planned an impromptu trip to Hiroshima for the next day - our final, full day in Japan.

An example of that is here. The plaque says the following, "In early August 1955, Sadako heard about an old saying, 'Fold 1,000 paper cranes and your wish will come true.' She began folding paper cranes using the small papers her medicine came in, and any other paper available. It is said that she folded 1,000 cranes in less than a month." This was heart-wrenching for me to read about how she passed away shortly thereafter. 

There was a section in the museum of items belonging to the victims of the bombings along with statements from their families. I sobbed throughout this entire display.
