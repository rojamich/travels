This morning offered a long boat tour where we saw a huge variety of birds! Potoo, turkey vultures, hawks, Kingfisher, yellow-hooded black birds,  bats napping and blending into the tree bark, and a heron munching on a fish! There are 1800 species of birds in the Amazon alone. We even saw another sloth enjoying some breakfast! An iguana was splayed out across a branch, enjoying the sunshine - so dragon-like! Mike caught me looking like a dork through the binoculars with my backward hat (so it won't fly off when the boat speeds up).

We went to the largest portion of the river, and both tour boats began doing donuts around the lake! This was to encourage the dolphins to play in the wake, which they love! We were able to see grey dolphins and the elusive pink dolphins (which are only in the Amazon!) It was so precious to see them so playful! We then stopped our boat and Moises jumped into the river - swimming time! I never thought we would swim in the Amazon?? Isn't that super duper dangerous?? Even a few days before, I would have said no freaking way! But, it looked fun, and it was hot and had been 3 hours since we saw a restroom, so swimming it was. We jumped in, and the water was so refreshing! We swam some laps, floated on the pool noodles, and Moises was very proud to explain that the river wasn't polluted; the cause of the coloring is the sediment bed it lies in.  


  


The afternoon we arrived, someone was standing out on the porch of the lodge, loudly talking on their phone (speakerphone) and walking where all the groups were quietly debriefing. Can you guess who it was? YES! The male half of the couple in the restaurant near Machu Picchu! We couldn't believe our luck that we were stuck with them in a location known for its connection to nature and solitude. 

We had spotted them in the airport, but they were going on another plane, so we had thanked our lucky stars we wouldn't see them again.

Luckily, we only have wifi here for 4 hours a day (Mike & I opted to stay unplugged for the Amazon portion of the trip, which was fantastic!)
