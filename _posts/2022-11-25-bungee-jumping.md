It was about a 2.5 hour drive down to Rotorua from Auckland but well worth the trip. We enjoyed dinner in Rotorua's downtown area called "Eat Street" which was a long corridor of restaurants with a lot of options to choose from.

After, we made it to our cozy cabin and anxiously thought about what we had in store for tomorrow...Bungee Jumping! Knocking another item off our bucket lists!

It had stormed every day of our trip thus far (and now, 10 days later, it still has!) But we read online that rain would not stop the jump, but storming would! So we were worried that it would be pushed back and our anxiety would only increase.

We woke up early and made the drive to Taupo for our Bungee Jumping appointment. We got in early and checked out the view we would get on the way down. It was dry and we were given the go ahead for the jump!

We measured our heart rates as we got closer to jump time and I went from 55bpm to 95bpm! Mike went from 82bpm to 102bpm!

We walked the platform out off the cliffs and it felt a bit like walking the plank! The instructor strapped us into our harnesses and blared pump up music. He was a calm, cool and collected dude. Mike and I were strapped together for a tandem jump and he had us penguin waddle over to the edge- the first time I felt any apprehension about jumping! We waved to the camera and placed our toes over the edge...WHY WERE WE DOING THIS AGAIN?!

I looked at Mike and heard the instructor chant, "JUMP IN THREE...TWO...ONE!" We both leaned forward and fell like a ton of bricks- screaming alllllll the way down.

  


  


The greenish/blue hue of the water was a really beautiful view to fall towards but I did not enjoy the second bounce after the initial fall.

That was easily the scariest experience of my life. To include skydiving. Just thinking about it, 6 days later now, makes my heart race unnaturally.
