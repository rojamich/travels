They do breakfast entirely different here. People line the counters like they’re lining up for shots. Which, I guess, they are. The café worker will present everyone with their coffee of choice. Usually espresso, or cappuccino. They will also have a small pastry with their beverage. After, the next row of people file in. This is around 730am. The worker then comes behind them and sweeps up all the pastry crumbs. It was all very rushed with a lot of hand gestures and forceful, hurried Italian. So, being tourists who speak predominately English, we disrupted this flow by asking if they spoke English (in Italian), and taking time to order each of our pastries and eating them at a table. I love breakfast and I love waking up early to slowly start the day and enjoy the peace so this city really screwed me up.

We had a nice walk over to the Colosseum until we got about 10 minutes away and realized I had forgotten the headphones. The best part about these sights have been Rick Steve gently explaining the history behind everything. It was really important that we get there before the crowd to take some good photos so we kept going.
