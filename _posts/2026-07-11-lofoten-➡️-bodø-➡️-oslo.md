Boy howdy this morning came early as we had (mostly) stayed up watching the Norway v. England game that went into overtime (called extra time in soccer). The game went til 2am so I napped most of the extra time. It was a sad game where Norway did not play their best.

Regardless, we sprung out of bed and made breakfast, packed, and hit the road for Jenna's second manual driving lesson. 

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783873923%2F20260712_094148_1_ewhvdf.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783873843%2F20260712_094154_kbmaga.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22Mike%20says%20this%20is%20my%20zen%20face.%22%7D %}

It went really well! I was able to shift and stop and get up to third! Of course, everyone and their mom decided to come to the same intersection at once so I got a smidge overwhelmed then. Its not fun driving manual when you have to give the right-of-way to everyone on your right when driving down the road. There are no stop signs in Lofoten, just this policy. 

But I felt good and appreciated my patient teacher! I don't understand why people enjoy driving manual but love it on a motorcyle. Probably the same reason I'm bad at soccer. Poor hand-foot coordination.

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783874065%2F20260712_095135_xkq6rd.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783874070%2F20260712_095128_intnuo.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

It was a one hour drive to Svolvær and a lot of RVs on the road. We made a stop at a cafe that was attached to a hostel. It was really cute and lots of greenery surrounding it! We hopped out of the car and were promptly greeted by a very excitable man waving at us (practically hopping with welcome!) We smiled and waved back, what a friendly hostel owner! 

The cafe was busy and filled with goodies and coffee. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783873543/20260712_103744_yfrb5f.jpg "Love this mindset!")

We grabbed Mikes coffee and headed back to the car where we saw the same man again outside. He jogged over to us and said HEY, happily and went in for a hug. I (Jenna) was surprised but man, he's so friendly! 

> Halfway into the hug he said, "OH, I don't know you!" 
>
> I said, "Nope!"
>
> He said "OH no, I'm sorry you just looked so familiar!"
>
> We all laughed and I said, "We thought you were just really friendly!"
>
> He laughed, clearly embarrassed, and said, "Well, I'm David!" and went in to shake Mike's hand.

I had to hold all of my laughter in as we walked away. My eyes were streaming with laughing tears but I held the hysterics in until we got in the car.

Finally, we dropped off the rental car and grabbed coffee and snacks (these were hard to find because it's Sunday and a lot of places are closed on Sundays here). But my hot caramel latte was really good! When Mike went to order, one of the girls at the counter was singing "put the lime in the coconut" and her coworkers were teasing her because no one knows that song. Mike confirmed he knew the song and she said, "It's from friends!" Apparently, people often use friends (the TV show) to learn English!

Our ferry ran during lunch and was 3.5 hours long. It was a "speedboat ferry". They immediately advised to stay seated as much as possible because we had poor weather which made for a rocky ride. 

{% youtube_block %7B%22url%22%3A%22https%3A%2F%2Fyoutube.com%2Fshorts%2FH1kGOGJHOpg%22%7D %}

We waved to "Fiskarkona" on our way out. She is a fisherman's wife who is worrying over her husband's safety and is a tribute to wives of fishermen in this community. Lofoten was built on fishermen. Not literally.

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783873549%2F20260712_123343_jtkzt8.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783875236%2F20260712_123340_debb4w.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783873601%2F20260712_123407_v3xorm.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

We were also able to see the drying racks for all the stockfish! Walking in this area on warm days is unadvisable due to smell. The locals say it "smells like money". 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783873617/20260712_123246_tcvbjg.jpg)

The ferry felt a bit long with children screeching and climbing on the seats. Thankfully, the snacks were plentiful and my book ([The Midnight Train](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/242380266-the-midnight-train)) is FABULOUS!!!

We landed and walked around Bodø - most of the town is shutdown thanks to Sunday but is much bigger than we expected.

Ramen was our choice for dinner and it tasted like dirty feet/bathwater.

We are working in a cafe until our flight tonight - it will take us to Oslo (only an hour and a half flight!) but getting in at midnight. Here's to a full nights sleep 🤞🏼
