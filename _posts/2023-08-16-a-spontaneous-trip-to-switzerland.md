Yes, after the casual mention of a day trip to Switzerland was made by our Airbnb host, we sprung into action to make it happen the next day.

What was originally going to be a relaxing trip to Lake Como was quickly demolished when Mike found an 8-mile hike and we found out another country was doable.

We woke up at 6am and started the trek over to the local bakery. We grabbed some bananas and pastries and Mike had an espresso. We took the first train to Lecco and then a transfer to Monza.

What was odd, this time none of our trains offered wifi. Thankfully our esim data lasted us the entire trip even when having to use navigation nearly every day.

On our final train to Lake Lugano, Switzerland. Police came onboard at the border to check passports. Thankfully we did not need a visa to enter (Switzerland is not part of the EU) so we had a moment of concern before looking it up. He questioned the group next to us who had really thick and colorful passports about where they were going and how long they would be there. Apparently, we were not concerning.

We found this odd orb, what looks like the head of the Statue of Liberty, and the oddest bathroom I've ever used! 

The bathrooms were hit-and-miss here with having to pay. Thankfully this one was free. You walk in and the toilet seat is attached to the wall, folded up about chest height with a metal handle. There's a large sink-shaped thing beneath it. You pull it down and have to hold it down with your weight to use it. Once you're done, you stand up (yes, it does smack you in the ass on the way back up) and automatically flushes once it returns to the up position. There is a tiny spout on the left-hand side that spits out soap and a hole that shoots water out and you wash your hands over the sink/toilet hole. So strange lol Mike said the seat looked like the shoulder guards on a roller coaster.

We found out the bus comes every 4 hours and only takes euros. We had an hour and a half before the next one so we trekked back down the hill and found a shady spot to lay and read and laugh about Shōgun. Now that we were both reading it.

After making it back up the hill, an express bus came by for our same route. It was about 40 euros more than ours and no stops- making it in half the time. We weighed the options and decided to stay with the 40-minute bus that was cheaper. Now we are glad we did! The roads are so narrow and our driver flew through them. Both of us get easily motion sick and were wearing motion-sick patches in anticipation for the ferry and still got nauseous! We can't imagine how bad it would've been if there were no stops! But the driver was excellent and seemed to be best friends with every driver on the road. Minus the motorcyclists who FLEW around cars and whipped around people with no regard for life or limb! We were glad we didn't rent motorcycles here.

  


We made it into Menaggio and set off to explore this new town. It was beautiful and the walk was rewarding.
