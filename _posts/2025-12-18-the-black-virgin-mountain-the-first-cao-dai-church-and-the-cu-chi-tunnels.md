We got an early morning workout in before our all-day tour this morning! I think we were too excited to sleep late because we were going to see the Cu Chi Tunnels today! Mike probably watched a dozen Vietnam War documentaries leading up to this trip. Every trip we go on, he absorbs the history to prepare - so nerdy and cute! Our tour guide (Aurora) arrived early, just as the coffee shop downstairs opened. We found out this was going to be a private tour - just the two of us! WHAT! What a wonderful surprise! No big van, no waiting for anyone, just us and our 100,000 questions!

We certainly got our money's worth this day. Mike began peppering Aurora with questions about the history, the country, and the Vietnamese culture. She seemed very excited to talk about all of it! A few things we learned are that HCMC - 327 years old, is a very young city compared to Hanoi - 1,015 years old (the capital). HCMC was also originally part of Cambodia's territory!

Aurora joked that there are two seasons in HCMC - hot season and hotter season! But seriously, there aren't four seasons here - there is just wet season and dry season. The wet season is typically May to November, but it's rained several times while we have been here - haha!

The major religions here are Buddhism (4-13%) and Christianity (7-8%), but the majority (around 86%) are not affiliated with a religion - how interesting!

After a two-hour car ride, we took a quick cable car ride up the mountain!
