This was our view flying into the toasty and dry Zimbabwe! The airport, however, does look futuristic! We were lucky to be one of the first off the plane. Another reason why we book trips so far in advance is to have the best shot at a good seat. We were in the front of the plane for this one! Customs was a madhouse and completely inefficient. It was so slow moving and there were no fingerprints taken or computer documenting. Just facial recognition which was interesting. We made it through to see there were no less than 50 taxi drivers out front battling for customers. After some quick research we determined the appropriate amount for our short ride and got a taxi to our hotel.

If you aren't familiar (like we weren't) Zimbabwe is in economic instability with things overinflated to the point that locals can no longer afford to live here and are seeking refuge in South Africa. So everything here is super expensive (relative to other countries in the vicinity) and similar to US costs. Many of the roads are unpaved in this area and have scam artists walking up and down them to take advantage of unsuspecting tourists. One such scam is trying to sell their crumbling currency for out of this world prices.

The next morning we were set to explore Victoria Falls, the tallest waterfall in the world! We stopped back into Marula for breakfast and found a table outside. At this point we saw baboons playing on the roof across the street. Next thing we know there is a loud THUMP above us on the tin roof of the outdoor seating! The baboon leader is now walking back and forth above us as we watch his ominous shadow on the wall beside us. 

The staff was not acting casual and walked over to us. We asked, "Should we move inside?" She nervously said, "Yes, I believe so." SCARY! 

We moved inside and this gentleman who worked next door came over to guard the door with a broom! WHAT IN THE WORLD.
