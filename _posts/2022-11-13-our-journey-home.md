We had to head back to the train station for our train back to London but on the way, I got some more chocolate and a bracelet that has the Italian flag on it! I love Amsterdam!

I do not love the process to get from Amsterdam to London. We walked up to the top of the train platform and got in line to go through security. They checked our COVID passes and tickets. 

Then we went through the security line and then onto customs. We got into the wrong line but thankfully they didn’t say anything. We had to explain where we had been and how long we had been at each place. We got a London stamp when we arrived there and now an Amsterdam stamp to leave! Then we stood in the customs line for London and showed our passports and explained everything again. Then we sat down in a super crowded room to wait with everyone else for the train. Unfortunately, it was very toasty and then we were on a packed train heading to London. We also didn’t account for the time change going back so the train ride was supposed to be 3.5 hours and it turned into 4.5 hours. Apparently, all of the loud people decided to get on this train.

When we arrived at the station, we had to take a metro to the main train station near the airport. We found the correct line and rode it to the main train station where we rushed off to find the best line to the airport. We had to get our covid documents checked prior to our flight the next day so we wouldn’t end up paying for a covid test or quarantining in London.

We hopped off the train and rushed to the main section of the terminal. Out of the corner of my eye, I was looking at the pastry shops for gingerbread cookies. It was the only thing we hadn’t accomplished in London before we left for Belgium and we didn’t have any time to go into the city to find some this time. Against all odds, I SAW ONE!!!! HOW???? It wasn't even Christmas time!?

We had an economy flight home on Virgin Atlantic and it was a rough one... people fighting, yelling and one guy who was oblivious to those around him. But it was great to get home and see our family before we made the final drive to Oregon!

  


Time for a New Chapter of Life!
