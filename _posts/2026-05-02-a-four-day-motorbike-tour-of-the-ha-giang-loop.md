Vietnam and China, on motorbikes, wahoo!

But, first, a 6-hour ride in a "VIP Limo Bus". The chairs lean back, and the footrest kicks up! We were on a bus with two girls from Kansas and a guy from India (but he lives in Dubai now).

We were all a little nervous when we stopped halfway and were ignored by our driver. The trip description said breakfast was included, but apparently, that only meant if you were being picked up from Sapa, not Hanoi. The food was terrible and pricey for Vietnam. We asked our driver, and he finally let us know he does not speak English and is not affiliated with the tour beyond transport... red flag #1 🚩 The rest of the drive was spent with him making various stops along the route, dropping random people off, picking them up, and transporting goods 

\- all the while, cash was exchanging hands, red flag #2 🚩. What an interesting and stressful start.

We arrived at the hostel where the motorbike portion of our tour was set to begin. We were all circled up, anxious for what came next. The lead guide (only English speaker) identified who would be going on a 3-day, 2-night tour and a 4-day, 3-night tour. Neither of the groups knew the other existed! We were then assigned drivers and our materials strapped down to the back of the bike! This was actually a pretty convenient backrest lol.
