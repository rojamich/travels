The uber on the way over to the historic district was a very aggressive driver so we were able to weave through traffic. Speaking of traffic, it is infamously awful here. Endless lines of cars fill the roadways and there seem to be traffic laws that are merely suggestions. Pedestrians, ambulance sirens, lanes, blinkers, and merging don't seem to mean much. People seem to go when they please and other people let them. We haven't seen any road rage here and it seems like people honk for fun? Not really used to illustrate any points. Mike says they honk at the rain for falling on the windshield.

There were spices, clothing, a lot of wrapping paper options, kids toys, snacks, etc.

We made it out of the market which spat us directly out to Chinatown! It was an odd Chinatown, like someone might have dreamed about a chinatown but never had visited one. There was a lot of food and toys there that were of no relation to china lol.

To get back to an uber-friendly area, we had to go through PACKED streets with random stalls of wares for sale. Mostly anything you can imagine was for sale there: knock-off jordans, ceiling fans, charging cords, giant lightbulbs, broken cellphones, speaker systems, paintings, bracelets, and so many other things I couldn't remember.
