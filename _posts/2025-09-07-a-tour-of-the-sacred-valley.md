Our time in Cusco had finally come to an end and we had an early wake up to tour the Sacred Valley! We got packed up, stomached some breakfast downstairs at the hotel, and poured some coca tea to prepare for an even higher elevation.

We brought the cup of tea to our lips as our guide strolled into the hotel lobby to pick us up. We enjoyed one sip of tea and headed out!

  


  


The first stop was a local village to get muña and coca tea for the elevation sickness. Mike and I were sat next to a kind tourist from Germany! We are always so excited to find a German tourist in every country we visit. They're always so well-traveled with a lot to talk about and Mike gets to break out his German language.

Next we were brought to a silver workshop where we learned about the different stones used in Peru. For example, Onyx is said to ward off bad energy. Chrysocolla stone is for health. You can see Mike and our German tourist friend, Max.  We had time to walk around the tiny town and enjoy a snack. We boarded the bus and waited. And waited. The guide and driver came on and got off the bus again. We noticed one woman was gone. I had seen her earlier but she had deboarded shortly after. They searched and called her with no response. 

The guide and driver boarded the bus, and left. A true nightmare for a female, solo traveler, it would be so scary to be left in a town all by myself!

Lunch was a delicious buffet where there was a llama lurking nearby! Honestly, that could be an alpaca. They have both and even after looking it up, we still can't tell the difference. 

  


  


Our final stop of the day was the Ollantaytambo Archeological site! The tour would return to Cusco but Mike and I were staying in Ollantaytambo for the night so we could continue on to Machu Picchu. Instead of carrying our bags with us into the site, we took a minor detour to drop them at our Inn. 

  


When we arrived, we quickly checked in and were given a key to our room. Unfortunately, we opened our room's door and an elderly gentleman greeted us The couple had mistakenly taken our room They wondered if we would just switch to make it easier? We were about to, when we realized ours was a totally different room type and price.
