Another amazing train ride. While it was more jerky, and less nice of a train, the actual scenery was brilliant! We went along the coast of Italy from Pisa, to Rome! The water is so turquoise and beautiful, I really hope we return to dive in the summer, some day. Towards the end of the ride, it began pouring and we had a lightening and thunderstorm streaking across our windows! It was really beautiful and inspiring to write to. I was able to finish documenting our whole trip to Florence on the train and relive its majesty.

When we arrived in Rome, it was pouring like no other! I love the rain! We were really in a rush because our Airbnb said they would charge a £30 fee for being later than 9pm. We arrived at the station at 745pm. We decided a 37-minute walk with our bags in the rain wasn’t a good idea so we caught an uber. 

We figured out where our Airbnb was after some floundering. We had to message the host while waiting in the rain to find out which apartment button to push. We were let in and walked up five flights of stairs (no elevator) to be met by an elderly couple that did not speak English. I tried to understand the best I could but, overall, this was not a great start. As of this writing, I have fallen going up and down the stairs and Mike fell going down the stairs and almost fell a few more times. Treacherous times I tell you!

And New York was our best comparison for Rome, with fewer amenities and skyscrapers. 

After settling in, we went in search for some good pasta and ended up at That’s Amore! It was about 930pm and we were ravenous. Thankfully it was only a 15-minute walk and it had stopped raining. We somehow got a table and a rush came in right after us!

    We both really enjoyed our dishes and. I ate it so quickly I forgot to take a before photo😆
