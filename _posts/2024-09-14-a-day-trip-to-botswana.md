After an hour's drive we went through several layers of customs to get out of Zimbabwe and into Botswana. One of those was a temperature check with paperwork asking if we had any symptoms of monkey pox (at this time, monkey pox is prevalent in the following countries:

Burundi, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Republic of the Congo (hereafter referred to as Congo), Côte d'Ivoire, DRC, Ghana, Liberia, Kenya, Nigeria, Rwanda, South Africa and Uganda.

  


Many of our co-tourists did not read English well and Mike called me the mom of the group all day for explaining, helping, taking photos for others, pointing out animals and bathroom opportunities lol. Hey, I'd want someone to do it for me!

  


We were then transferred to our tour guide for the land safari portion named "Cobra". I had read several online reviews praising him for his guidance but he was not a good guide in our experience. He drove like we were flying (we passed all the other touring vehicles) on massively bumpy (sand) roads. All of us were holding onto the vehicle and getting carsick, not to mention the sand being kicked up everywhere.
