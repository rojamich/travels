Some things we wish we had done/known for Belgium.

At least two days for Bruges alone would have been perfect for me. I freaking loved it there because it was all so beautiful and older. The town was relatively quiet most of the time and it would have been nice to stay and explore its nooks and crannies. "When you’re there, it feels like a fairytale. You know you’re awake but you feel like you’re dreaming."

The driving in Belgium was very calm and everyone was on the right side of the road. Pedestrians, cyclists and autos were all respectable of one another and clear driving rules. 

The COVID restrictions were not enforced but the Passenger Locator Form was scanned on arrival. Masks were generally always enforced in shops, transportation, and restaurants. You had to be fully vaccinated to come here. 

My EE Sim card worked well in this town and LEBARA did not work very well. Our Airbnb wifi was poor.

City Mapper was, again, more beneficial at most times over google maps. I think there was only one circumstance we needed google maps. 

We had to use the trains here mostly but I LOVED them. Thankfully, those employed with the train station were very helpful when we were confused about where to go.

The water in the restaurants costs to drink, it does not come with your meal for free. The bathrooms are for charge only. Some restaurants allowed you to use them if you ate there. 

The walking was much more organized here. Everyone walks on the right side of the sidewalk. 

Days visited: February 11th-13th.

Daily weather: Low 30s in the morning and evenings, Low 40s in the middle of the day.

Total distance walked: 16.73 miles

Transportations used: train, uber, and walking.
