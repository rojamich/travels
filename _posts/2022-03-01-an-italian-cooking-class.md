The next morning, we slept in and woke to eat more cannoli. If they had protein, I could probably live off of them. I have tried cannoli before and did NOT like them. Italy is so special. 

    But today was about to be amazing. I was _SO_ excited for our cooking class! I found it online on recommended things to do in Italy and loved the idea of learning how to cook Italian food, with Mike, _in_ Italy! Can you picture something more perfect? The only thing I can think of that’s _even_ close would-be Valentines Day in Paris… OH WAIT! We already did that!

    Once we arrived, we were met by a vivacious and charismatic Italian chef who teaches all over, owns the cooking class company, and will be in California teaching at professional cooking academies for three months this year! We were introduced to the menu and I was in shock! It all sounded like food I would eat! Three different types of pasta and a dessert I had no idea what it was but the recipe sounded delicious! Each pasta was from a different region of Italy. One of my favorite parts was every time the Chef instructed on chopping, he would say “choppy choppy choppy” He said sometimes it was easier to say things with sound effects lol. 

The most fun part was learning how to make the pasta! It was really cool to use the rolling machine! We made our own Gnocchi (which I was thrilled to see was on the menu), fettuccini, and ravioli! Mike and I got a lot of enjoyment out of helping each other make the different types of pasta.

After what seemed like four hours (hey! I was hungry) we sat down to eat. We tried all three types of pasta, the ravioli being our favorite (we were both surprised!) It was covered in olive oil, stuffed with cheese and spinach and really sparked our tastebuds! We couldn’t get enough. 

The gnocchi was our second favorite. The mix of potato and garlic was sublime. Lastly, the fettucine, the “sauce” was really good! The mixture of meats and veggies was unique in a really good way! The noodles were so soft and we were struck by how amazing it was to have everything so truly fresh. My heart is alive thinking about how good the food was and that _we_ made it!

I had nearly forgotten about dessert (who me??) when he brought out the panna cotta covered in blueberry sauce. Oh my gosh it was delightful! It was our first time trying it and the cream base combined with the sweet blueberries (usually don’t like a blueberry straight to the mouth) was a really satisfying.
