The lobby was a peaceful oasis at this time of morning and we decided to read a bit by the fire before moving on to our first stop of the day!

  


The day prior, Mike had spotted a cable car next to our hotel! It turns out, that led to the very point we had plotted to see Mt. Fuji! It opened at 830am so we headed over to get there early at 8am. As we rounded the corner, my heart sank. There was a line forming quickly! We hopped in it and hoped we weren't too late. Our shuttle to the train station was leaving at 930am! We didn't even have tickets for the cable car yet! As the line inched closer and closer to the front my anxiety grew and the line to buy tickets was down to the street. We mentally hurried the crowd in front of us and managed to get tickets for the cable car.

  


We raced up the (several) flights of stairs and got in line - hoping like heck we were going to make the first car! We were the very last couple to hop into the cable car and, boy howdy, it moved fast!

We made it to our first train and enjoyed our final views of Fuji while reading more 💓 

  


We made it to our connection in time to get the bullet train ticket. Unfortunately, this is where the day took a turn. I didn't realize when you insert the ticket into the turnstile, you have to be ready to snatch it back when it spits it out at the other end! I turned to look back at Mike and the machine sucked my ticket back in! To make matters worse, the fare was a split fare since we were taking three different train lines! We were lucky enough to come across the kindest and speediest station employee who got us the correct fare, refunded the incorrect one, and instructed us on how to mitigate my missing ticket when we arrived in Kyoto. Did I mention that she and Mike managed this with Japanese and a translation app?? What an ordeal. This dampened the day a bit since we weren't sure if we would have to pay a second time for the fare but we tried to soak up our next couple of train rides anyway!

As you can see, we are still very excited about bullet trains!

We arrived in Kyoto and had to transfer to a local line. Unfortunately, this meant dealing with a rather unpleasant station employee which gave us a frustrating first impression of Kyoto - everyone else had been nothing but kind and patient the entire journey thus far! While he spoke down to us like the scum we were, I watched a little old lady fail to move fast enough to snatch her ticket from the machine as well. I guess it's not just me!

  


We found the fare adjustment office and braced ourselves for the worst (a major fee). The man listened patiently to our story (in Japanese) and gave us a kindly smile while he handed us a new ticket like it was nothing and no problem at all. We decided we would look into adopting him as our grandfather.
