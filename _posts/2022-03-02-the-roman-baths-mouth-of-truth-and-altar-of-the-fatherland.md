The next morning was a beautiful day and we stopped by the near-empty Trevi fountain to get a good look without the crowd. It was worth it!

 We hurried over to the Roman baths. I was trying my best to avoid that metro again and had not yet recovered from the traumatic bus ride from Belgium so we walked the entire way- a mistake lol. We got there entirely too early and had to hike a bit to find a breakfast place. We got a few delicious pastries and Mike got his espresso. He says this country will ruin him when he tries to go back to regular coffee at home lol. We thought there would be a lot more to the baths. There was no Rick Steve’s tour so we tried out an audio tour from the front desk and it was on an old Samsung phone with no headphones and we didn’t have any! It was entirely too quiet and virtually repeated the same thing the signs said. Which was more about the actual construction of the baths than their actual use. We did learn that they had a library inside the baths and that sometimes, 6,000 to 8,000 people attended the baths _every day_!

On our way back to our place, we walked around the Circus Maximus which used to be a racetrack! Part of it was blocked out for a VR session you could pay for but we stuck with enjoying a walk. Something I was really looking forward to was the Mouth of Truth, which I thought would have water dribbling out of it! According to legend, if a liar places his hand in its mouth, or attempts to lie with your hand in its mouth, it will bite you!
