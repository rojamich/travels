This led us to the funniest part of our effing tour!! We walked into a beerhall that was the “world’s most famous” according to our audiotour! Didn’t seem like much to us but we were willing to give it a try! The mashed potatoes with bacon were fantastic and I really liked my potato soup! Mike is having his first German Bratwurst!

As we left, we saw the actual most famous beerhall, The Hofbräuhaus! We laughed our butts off at how we mistakenly ate at the wrong place! The audio guide described it as loud from the music and full of crazy drinkers! No wonder this was nothing like our experience and the beerhall was supposed to be three stories tall!

We walked to the Max-Joseph-Platz which is a big square with a statue of King Maximillian I.

Leaving the square, we were guided to an alleyway a block over which holds Viscardigasse. The gold-colored cobblestones illustrate the path taken by those hoping to avoid saluting at the Odeonsplatz (a block over). Saluting at this memorial was required, those not subscribing to the ideology would take this path to evade it.
