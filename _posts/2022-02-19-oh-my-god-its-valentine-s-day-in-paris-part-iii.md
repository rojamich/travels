Past Jenna would never believe what we did next! We had a six-course meal at a fancy restaurant in Paris on Valentines Day! And I tried every, single thing! 

The menu was as follows:

Course 1

Chicken Liver Mousse with Bread – Holy crap this was amazing!!!  I _never_ thought I would like liver???

Chestnut Veloute, Onions Cream Soup

This soup was unlike any other soup. It was brilliant. Here is a photo of me being astonished by how they served it! There were all the delicious toppings in the bowl by itself and they bring the pitcher of soup and fill your bowl in front of you!!

Course 5 – the best course

Almonds and Hazelnutes cream, dark chocolate and vanilla ice cream!

Course 6 – in the same photo

Rice  Pudding with vanilla and salted caramel sauce

I’ve been routinely trying tree nuts to see if my allergy is relieved and almonds still make my mouth slightly itchy but no longer swelling! Hooray! The ice cream bowl was freaking good! The pudding was weird but the caramel was wonderful and what I have been needing in my life.

Coffee shops with syrups are VERY hard to find in Paris! Apparently, people like the taste of coffee here??

  


![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEhvB-PVPhYlRwL0NJn4kD5jk4TTYJtEEmlEUfxNIP6DqivWuJ9CglAaGURe7WtCu84FXZ2nOzmkFFo_LFY7_S-RaFk0eDDTxMj5KdP5MdvdQo0_HA-Ddzfh7w-7mhkJ6fDRaP6qimbENaS91TNcoksf07eXReEQEDwT-bII3hG7zzJNUNqfMq-unOt8=s320)

  


The overall experience of the six-course meal really took us by surprise. We typically do not enjoy very fancy food and this really knocked our socks off! The waitresses were very sweet and professional and the courses were brought out based on how quickly we ate the previous one. We really inhaled that soup, it was so good! The restaurant was beautiful and we felt very uptown! Being able to try so many new things and share that with Mike was really special. It definitely changed our perspective on those types of meals.

We ended the day with a beautiful view of the Eiffel Tower 💓
