We continued walking down the streets of Brussels when we heard a marching band start up! We headed towards it and found them at the square by the Grand Place! There we saw a large statue being marched around the square! Following the statue was a group of older men in the same outfit, a marching band, and a huge crowd!

People were singing, dancing and celebrating! We had no idea what we had walked into but we followed them and discovered they were heading to our next stop, Manneken Pis! This is where we leanred that this is a display of a regular festival that takes place in Brussels.

This website illustrates the importance of the statue and its history in Brussels: [www.brussels.be/manneken-pis](http://www.brussels.be/manneken-pis). I thought it was really cool that it was vital to the distribution of drinking water in the 15th century. During this festival, the new outfit of the statue is revealed. This occurs approximately 23 times a year and the outfits number over 1000!

 How lucky are we that it happened on a day we were there!!?? It appears this outfit has the little boy dressed as a chocolate maker! His tiny pocket has an even tinier piece of chocolate in it. The even stranger part of this celebration was when the statue began “peeing” on the crowd and those being peed on where cheering in excitement?? Michael was disappointed he was not chosen to be peed on…
