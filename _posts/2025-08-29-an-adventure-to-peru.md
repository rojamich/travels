Welcome back everyone!

Sometimes, the adventure happens when you arrive, sometimes the adventure is just getting there!

We had an easy flight from Portland to Houston, managed to grab coffee and food in Houston despite a less than ideal airport - thanks a lot Houston >:(

But we made our connection and boarded the plane. We had epic seats! We each had the spot in the corner of the exit row where we had no seats in front of us so we had ultimate leg room!!

I had a very strange man in the row ahead of me though. The first thing he did when he sat down was turn all the way around, stare at me until I looked at him and he said, "I wanted your seat. I'm very tall." He was very cranky and very tall. He then proceeded, over the next  hours, to lay his head on the stranger next to him, grab the mans chair in front of him, step on my bag, took a few tokes off his vape pen, and eventually yell and curse at individuals who didn't understand instructions given over the intercom (only in english).

  


About two hours into our flight from Houston to Peru, the pilot announces there is a software issue in the plane that doesn't allow them to see through the clouds...we will have to return to Houston. So we fly back to Houston and have to fly in circles for awhile to burn off the fuel :o.

  


When we land, thankfully they have rebooked us on the next morning's flight, the soonest one to Peru, sent us meal vouchers, and booked us in a nearby hotel. We hurry to call the hotel shuttle and Mike snagged us upgraded seats on the flight, YAY!!!

After a near full night's sleep, we get back to the airport around 530am, grab breakfast and coffee so we can do this all over again :o

Thankfully our seats were awesome with leg rests and I actually slept about four out of five hours of that flight!!

The only good things about the flight delay were that I finished reading two books and the first few hours of our first day in Lima were scheduled to be beach day time, so we didn't miss much of actual Lima.

  


We caught a taxi at the airport (here its the cheapest way to get to our area of the city and highly regulated). Our driver gave us a mini tour of the area and was really excited to show us the ocean! It was pretty cute. We made it to our hotel, freshened up to head to a massage (just in time!) I was given a very small woman who was shaking when she applied pressure to my shoulders but it was a very relaxing massage!
