The next morning, we woke up and got going about 8am, not ready for the 24 degree weather that awaited us! Oh man I am not sure I’ve regained feeling in my fingers yet! 

Of course, there was a Munich walking tour led by the Rick Steve's audio guide that led us around town! I love the gothic style City Hall and it reminded me of some of the architecture in Belgium! Rick Steve said the clock on the new City Hall building would chime and dance at 11 so we continued on, planning to return.

As we walked, we learned that Munich was founded by Monks which gave it its name, München. The city’s symbol is also a little monk, how cute!

After standing outside of a nearby cathedral for far too long, we realized which church we were supposed to be at (around the corner), and we entered St. Peter’s church.

The first thing that struck us was the skeletal remains of a woman named Munditia. She was killed for being a Christian and the church honored her and displays her to educate others. If you are killed for your faith, you go straight to heaven, no waiting around for judgement day.

We were led to the market place that is usually packed with Octoberfest goers because it will serve seven traditional types of beer here! The area was called the Viktualienmarkt and we were supposed to find a Maypole that had ribbons and directions. After searching for a bit, we finally asked a local who advised it had been removed since COVID. There were a few cool shops here that we later returned to for honey, pretzels, and cheese! It was really Obatzda cheese and Mike liked it a lot too!

We continued on to Eatly. Which is an Italian eatery that has a chocolate store, winery, grocery store, books, pastries, and gelato!!?? This is heaven!
