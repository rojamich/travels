The sky was bright when I woke up this morning, asking Mike, "Did it ever get dark here?" Summer solstice is 3 days away so sunset is at 1030pm here! Sunrise is at 330am! Holy moly! We will be using the shades tonight!

There's a lovely park downstairs so I headed out to dance :)

I found a drip coffee for Mike and decided today would be my first day sans coffee (wish me luck!) I made it back and we made eggs and rice for breakfast - yum! After a shower, I was pretty tired and took a 2.5 hour nap! All that lack of sleep finally caught up with us and we ended up sleeping 10.5 hours total :o

The grocery store around the corner (literally on this block!) had a delicious looking salad bar that I was stoked for! This was our lunch :) and it was so good!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1781809350%2F20260618_131236_qy9elj.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1781809350%2F20260618_133658_kcl2yr.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

Finally, the latest we have explored a city... we set out at 2pm to walk and discover Oslo! Our first stop took us through this park nearby (yes, the fourth nearby park) which held sculptures of children's artwork - come to life! How neat is that!?

They marched back and forth in the sun. We got to see one of them yell at a tourist who wasn't paying attention and walked across the boundary onto the palace grounds!

King Karl Johan looks out over the city from atop a horse on Palace grounds! Check out the view too!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781810408/20260618_145325_qklgcj.jpg "Many of the sites along this street were featured in the Jo Nesbo series that inspired our trip here! We must re-read them to compare!")

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781810621/20260618_145921_f4hjv7.jpg "This is Norway's national theatre! It was built in 1899 and is still used today!")

Right across the street was the University of Oslo - this is where the Nobel Peace Prize was awarded until 1990 before it moved to Oslo City Hall. Graduation season is upon us so the area was rife with tourists and graduates alike! I was reminded of the rules in Europe surrounding smoking and how packaging of these items have strict regulations!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781810811/20260618_145950_w4nei7.jpg)

The architecture here is blowing my mind! This looks like it should be a tower related to a dracula novel - not a restaurant / business offices.

If you haven't noticed, Oslo has a major support for pride month and displays pride flags nearly everywhere - quite the contrast to London where those flags were scarce - Happy Pride Month!

We took a quick break to explore a bookstore nearby while Mike tried a protein shake packed with fruit (he says Yum!!)

This shop was not well-air-conditioned but it was filled with books I've never seen or heard of! I even tried looking them up on Libby and amazon and most were unattainable! Many people here speak English but we have encountered a few that don't! It was also playing the soundtrack to my best friends wedding - Love It!

One of the final exhibits in the museum is about a topic I'm passionate about and created/teach a class on - Communication!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781811982/20260618_164517_mee1tm.jpg "So many issues could be solved by good communication, it frustrates me to think about!")

We spent an hour at the museum and really enjoyed it! It reminded us a lot of the exhibit on the Pulitzer prize winners that often highlighted conflict and highly emotional happenings. We got to visit this exhibit twice at the Newseum in DC before it was shut down. You can see it [here](https://newseumed.org/tools/lesson-plan/pulitzer-prize-photographs-exhibit-guide) though!
