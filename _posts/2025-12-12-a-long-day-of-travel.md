We woke up at 3am like it was Christmas morning on the day of our flight! We didn't have to wake up until 4am, but we have been looking forward to this trip for a year! After re-packing our bags, why did I think I needed jeans for a trip to the land of perpetual humidity and excellent street food? We left unnecessary items in the trunk of our car and headed to the airport! 

We arrived at the airport about an 1.5 hour early, but because we were traveling internationally, we had to get our boarding passes confirmed by the front desk - a massive line! Thankfully, there was a separate line for us - otherwise would've been waiting about an hour! We thanked our lucky stars that we had the clear option at the airport this morning! Usually, we just use pre-check, but we had a credit on one of our cards that allowed us to try out this fast-pass feature and skipped all the lines. We have never seen the airport that busy before!

After grabbing coffee and a light breakfast, we headed to our first flight to SFO - just a little one hour, warm up flight ;) 

San Francisco was a total win! We had a pass to the Admiral club and were able to kickback and spread out. Not to mention the snacks! We had avocado toast with eggs, prosciutto, and salmon (well, Mike had the meats lol). Yogurt, coffee, tea, crackers, and fruit filled us up nicely while we read and charged our devices for our upcoming, long flight.

I need to make some avocado toast at home sometime! Because we booked through a partner airline, we didn't get real-time updates from the app. Luckily, we had our boarding passes printed and could see flight time was 1140. We decided to leave the lounge a little early with enough time to get snacks and water ahead of the flight. As we packed up to leave, we received an email stating boarding had begun for our flight😮! We were so used to looking at the app that displays the boarding time, we mistook the printed flight time for the boarding time! We rushed to gather our things and hustled to the gate (which ended up being much further away than we anticipated). That's when Mike and I heard the announcement "Final boarding call" for the first time in our lives! 10/10 would not recommend. Thankfully, we were not the last to board, but we felt bad as the staff were not happy. Not a fun experience. 

  


We flew economy this time and selected seats across the aisle from each other so neither of us had to sit in the middle seat. We were still able to chat and help each other (holding items when we had to adjust or go to the restroom). Mike was seated next to an adorable, tiny, Asian woman. I was seated next to Mike's dad... if Mike's dad was Laotian. Super sweet, friendly, and giggly, we talked about traveling, his family, and tattoos. 

  


This was our 13-hour flight - we were supposed to sleep a lot to stave off the jetlag. Unfortunately, we both only slept about 2 hours. We did get a lot of reading done! I started and finished a book on this flight, which was fun! Mike finished his first Stephen King novel and started another Jo Nesbo book!

This flight had some solid food!

We were fed 3 times on this flight! Pork, rice, and a lot of boiled and steamed veggies! It was actually really good! The flight attendant showed us which items were to be mixed and drizzled. The random snack was a Hot Pocket XD. A passenger asked what it was, and the flight attendant described it as a ham and cheese burrito! It was a pepperoni hot pocket, haha. 

  


We were given slippers, and it was very relaxing not to have shoes on for that lengthy 13-hour flight. The bathrooms were stocked with toothpaste, toothbrushes, and face lotion. Just some awesome touches that made this flight doable!

  


We made it to Seoul and over to our connecting flight with enough time to grab some coffee to keep us up to avoid jetlag! Eating well, staying hydrated, and sleeping according to your upcoming sleep schedule have been our usual go-tos!

This 5-hour flight was a good one from Seoul to Ho Chi Minh City (HCMC)! We had a whole row to ourselves and spread out. I snuck in a 30-minute nap, started and finished another book. The food was great again! Pork bulgogi and a piece of strawberry cake?! Spoiled!

We landed early, 10:30pm, and hurried to security. We splurged on the fast-pass to get into Vietnam since customs and immigration can take up to 2.5 hours sometimes! After 19 hours of flying, no thank you! We only waited about 20 minutes, were asked no questions, and headed downstairs.

  


Our card also gave us a chauffeur credit, which meant a man was waiting downstairs with our name on an iPad to take us to our hotel! The seats were so comfortable, and the air conditioning was welcome when we were hit with humidity the moment we stepped outside.

  


Reception was ready and waiting for us downstairs (their normal waiting area is a flight up since our hotel has a coffee shop at the bottom!) We were quickly up in our room and crashed for the evening in our wonderful, air-conditioned room.
