Doom and Gloom indeed! It poured all night and into the morning as we made the two hour drive up to Mt. Doom. We looked at each other and just knew our luck had probably run out as every time we stepped out of the car, the rain stopped, but not this day. It had finally caught up to us. 

We arrived at Tongariro National Park and it was still storming, we watched another couple approach the hike entrance and turn back (probably a good idea) but we were overly stubborn and continued through the marsh-like, fog-covered land.

We were drenched from head to toe and our feet made a squelching noise inside our boots with every step. It took four days for our boots to finally and completely dry!

We blasted that car heater as we drove up to Gollum's pool.

Still not completely thawed out, we made the short hike down to the falls that flowed into Gollum's pool.
