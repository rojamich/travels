Carving our own jewelry was pretty high on the excitement list for this trip. It was fairly unique to this area and pretty cool we would have a natural but unique souvenir from our time here!

  


The first thing the instructor did was ask about Mike's heritage! He replied that his family was originally from Mexico which brought an awed look to our instructor's face and she explained she desperately wanted to see the beaches there and had friends who had visited that country! We enjoyed the refreshing perspective!

  


The first step in the carving process was to choose your stone. It had to be the right size and of course color was important. 

  


We discussed our choices with the instructor who was wary of me trying to make Mike a ring. He still wears his original ring from 10 years ago so I was thrilled for the opportunity to make him a new one! Apparently, it is very easy to break them and hard to make! I settled on a water droplet design to represent my love of overseas travel. 

  


Next, we moved to a sander and started to form the shape of our design in the rock. Making the rock circular was the most challenging part for me. No sharp edges and it had to stay big enough to fit his finger.

I was using his current ring as a comparison and set it on the table while I sanded. Unfortunately, the table was vibrating and the ring slipped off the table and rolled somewhere that six of us just could not find! Searching under the table, between grates, behind and under rocks...nothing!

After about 15 minutes we had to continue our projects while I stewed in my mistake! I finished sanding slightly before Mike and went back on the hunt. Although I thought unlikely, I went to the other end of the workshop and to the very back grate where I couldn't see, only feel and BAM!!! IT WAS THERE! THANK GOODNESS!!! I was so relieved as was the instructor. 

  


Next, we moved onto the smoothing process. Mike sat down with sandpaper to get an even finer surface while I was directed to the tool that allowed me to drill the hole for his finger.

It took a lot of focus to make sure the hole was the exact right size and all the interior edges were smooth, even, set in the middle and circular! It was fun to be creative and also make him something that was meaningful.
