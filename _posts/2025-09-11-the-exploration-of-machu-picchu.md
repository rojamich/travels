We woke up to this beautiful view, and I had SO many mosquito bites! We left the windows open for the breeze that night and didn't know there would be mosquitoes at this location. Regardless, we had a spring in our step with excitement that the day was finally here! We were eager to finally see one of the seven modern world wonders!

  


I decided a run was in order (despite not bringing running shoes), walking shoes would have to do. What I didn't anticipate were the bus lines! So many people were anxiously awaiting the bus from Aguas Calientes that would take them up to Machu Picchu. You have to buy the tickets the day before and then wait to be transported up, about an hour before your entry time. Word is, they are big sticklers about the entry times at the site! I flew down the hill, weaving through the crowd and over the bridge, and started my ascent up the other side of the hill. It wasn't bad the first go round!

Then I started up another street, careful to avoid the bus road, and suddenly my lungs and throat were just on FIRE. I was not prepared for its steepness or the elevation. It felt so good to be back running, though! Mike was kind enough to watch me from the top of the hill to ensure I was safe. I found this statue of me when I'm full. Just kidding, it's Mother Earth lol

  


We packed up and headed downstairs for some hotel breakfast. It wasn't bad, I enjoyed the quinoa mix with spicy salsa, and even the pancake was good!

The bus ride was an easy one with gorgeous views! The drivers were seemingly experts at navigating the winding turns with steep drop-offs. 

Disembarking the bus, we had to wiggle our way through the crowds in the warm sunlight. We weren't sure who our guide would be and had to look around for someone who happened to be looking for Michelle Rojas. That's right, they pronounce Mike's name as Michelle here - which is why he goes by Miguel lol. I found a spot in the entry line while Mike located our soft-spoken guide, almost whispering "Michelle Rojas". She (Judy) was less than 5 feet tall, and we got her in line with us. Once through, she ensured we didn't need the restroom, water, or sunscreen. Unfortunately, we had planned for cold, not bringing the sunscreen that was at the hotel. She gave us some of her stash, how kind!

Machu Picchu, she advised, means grandfather mountain. She showed us the Incan face outlined by the mountain as well! It was really cool that she had laminated photos to show us what she was explaining. We also learned that the mountain is all granite, naturally, and sits on a quarry! They brought all the soil in from Cusco through the Inca Trail via llamas! The Incas also chose this location because it is at the edge of the cloud forest and was excellent for fortification.

The conquistadors never found Machu Picchu. They didn't go further into the cloud forest because those who resided there practiced cannibalism. What do I mean by cloud forest? There is Duality Mountain just across from Machu Picchu, which is typically covered in clouds and has consistent rain. Other mountains were too steep to build a community in.
