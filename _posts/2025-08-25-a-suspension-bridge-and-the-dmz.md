The next stop was a sad one at Imjingak Pyeonghoa-Nuri Park. This park was originally built for those who were unable to return to their hometowns, family, and friends on the other side of Korea because of the division between North and South. Many come here to mourn and leave letters to loved ones.

We eagerly boarded the bus for the highlight of the day...the DMZ! The DMZ is a demilitarized zone between North and South Korea. It cannot be occupied or contain military activity, but is HEAVILY guarded. It is 160 miles long and 2.5 miles wide.

There are many tunnels dug below the DMZ by North Korea in an attempt at a surprise invasion of South Korea. We were able to walk down into one of these tunnels, but photography was restricted.

A museum of sorts is at the entry of the tunnel, where you could take photos and read inscriptions after walking deep into the tunnel. We had to wear hard hats going down there, and it was a SERIOUS hike back up. Despite the cold, you are sweating coming back up! This one is called the Third Tunnel, and you are crouched down for part of the journey inside. They originally located this tunnel in 1978! It was really humbling to see what other countries have gone through in recent history.
