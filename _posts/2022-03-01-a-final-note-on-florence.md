Some things we wish we had done/known for Florence.

I could probably live in the outskirts of Florence. If Mike worked from home and I was some famous author, I would jump at the chance. 

It is nearly impossible to drive in Florence without losing your mind. If you look at the photo of Florence at 4pm, _everyone_ walked in the streets there. The sidewalks were a laughable two feet wide and the city was teaming with tourists. Would not drive there if you paid me. Even the cyclists and motorcyclists could barely get a few feet without stopping. 

COVID vaccination was mandated. Italy uses a green pass but others are accepted. Our waiter told us that our card would be allowed from California but everywhere we went, they wanted to scan our QR code. We didn’t want to have to deal with the likely hassle of explaining our card everywhere with potential language barriers so we are very grateful for having the European Healthpass. No one ever checked our passports coming from Paris into Italy.

Both the Labara SIM card and the EE SIM card worked well here! Google maps was once again useless and we were using citymapper here. Walking was the only way to get around efficiently but everything was in very short walking distance! Something to remember in every city thus far is that the street signs are on the building. Italy, by far, has the most complex and interwoven streets! 

The water in the restaurants costs to drink, it does not come with your meal for free. The bathrooms are few and far between.

The diversity of languages here was vast thanks to the number of tourists! Sitting in restaurants, you would hear languages from all over the world! 

Days visited: February 18th\- 21st

Daily weather: High 50s and, some days, 60s!

Total distance walked: 16.79 miles

Transportations used: walking.
