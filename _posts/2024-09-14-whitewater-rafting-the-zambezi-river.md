We woke up and enjoyed this lovely view from our room before jumping into the day! We had another early morning pick up but this time it was to whitewater raft the Zambezi river (where Victoria flows into).

  


Unfortunately, this is the only photo I took this day because I didn't want to risk losing my phone! We had a short van ride into the national park, followed by a hike down the gorge - so steep!

  


Our guide was JB who was very knowledgeable and skilled. We were paired with a business man from Israel and a couple from Thailand! We were all enthusiastic and excited to see what the day would have in store for us!

  


We climbed aboard our raft and practiced the commands JB would issue as we went down the river. Things like forward, get down, and jumping out to swim! 

  


I was seated directly behind Mike and matched his row stroke to ensure the most efficient rowing. We went over category 2-5 rapids! It was insane to watch the drop on some of them where we felt like we were coming down over a waterfall! Other times we were tossed close to the rocks but JB expertly guided our rowing to avoid them.

  


About 20 minutes into rafting, JB allowed us to jump in and swim - it was so refreshing and not cold at all! The sun wasn't beating down like I thought it might so it was pretty perfect. I barely even had to put effort into swimming because the current was so strong. 

  


Once back in the boat, we learned that crocodiles and hippos inhabit this water. WHAT! How do you tell us this afterwards?

  


We were thrilled going over a category 5 rapid and all high-fived with our oars to celebrate. JB had us pull the boat over to watch some other groups go over it and we watched two people get launched from one boat and another raft flipped completely upside down!

  


It was such a thrilling day and we had about 5 opportunities to swim total. To finish off the experience, we had to get back to the top of the gorge, no elevator, just a steep and exhausting hike! But they said food was waiting for us at the top so we hustled- passing all other groups to get there lol

  


Once at the top, omg it was so steep, did I mention that? The food was not there yet lol

The food arrived about 20 minutes later when the rest of our group made it up and asked us if we flew up lol 

Hey you tell me I have a shot at food after some exercise and I'll move heaven and earth to get there. 

The food wasn't bad and we were both able to eat a full meal finally. 

  


We had a short van ride back to the hotel where we had a measly dinner and I did a hiit workout using our hotel room furniture (it was getting dark and I was nervous about the hogs and baboons).

  


Today was our last full day in Zimbabwe and I was looking forward to feeling better and getting some real hearty food at our next spot, Johannesburg !
