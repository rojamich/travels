This promised to be a full day adventure starting with a 5am pickup from our hotel. We had snagged lots of snacks for this day as they warned us there would be no breakfast stop. Per the usual, we were in a country that doesn't typically do breakfast! 🥞 🍳 

We climbed onto the spacious and well-airconditioned bus that even had footrests! Am I easily pleased? Maybe lol.

The night prior, we donned our scopolamine patches to prevent motion-sickness. These work like a charm every time we go diving as boats regularly make us sick, and we weren't sure how the driving would be for a long a day as well. The only downside to these patches is that we get sooooo sleepy. This was especially true today, I passed out constantly. On the bus, on the boat, on the sand dunes (just kidding on this one, the dune buggies were wild! lol).

  


We started the tour with a three and a half hour drive from Lima to Paracas - a coastal city where we would have a boat tour - This meant a wonderfully long nap, interspersed with podcasts for fantasy football. When we arrived, we had 10 minutes for the restroom and coffee, if we chose to spend the time this way. The bathroom was one soles for usage which is 28cents in USD. We found a coffee shop but the coffee wasn't what I hoped (this is typical when we are in other countries). If you know me, you know I love sugar, not coffee ☕

It was time for us to gather and head to the boats! These boats, and most other things here, are constructed for much shorter people. Mike and I have felt **very** tall here. Mike is 6'1, and I am 5'7 which is tall by US standards. The average height for males in the US is 5'9 and females is 5'4. 

In Peru, the average height for male is 5'5 and female is 5'0. So, when we boarded the boat, this was the leg room situation. When we received massages here, our feet hung off the tables as well. I could not help but to quietly laugh with surprise!

While waiting to leave, we ate our pre-ordered pasta while looking out over this beautiful view. Here is where we tried two more must-haves of Peru. The Inka Cola beverage, which tastes like bubblegum, and alfajor cookies which were so freaking soft 😱

  


It was another four hour drive back to Lima - another lovely nap. The tour guide arranged for an uber to take us from their drop off spot to our hotel. We got in after midnight and poured about a pound of sand each from our pockets, socks, and shoes. I'm not sure if I mentioned this but the water here is not potable. We have to use bottled water for consumption, including brushing our teeth. We can still shower and wash our hands with it but you need to be careful when you wash your face. Thankfully, we slept well this evening after a really fun day of touring Peru 🇵🇪🌴🚍
