We woke up in high spirits this day, ready to see what Johannesburg had in store for us! We were also ready for more various food options and a shower that worked properly. The shower head did not work so we had to use the handle to rinse ourselves which felt like bird baths lol

  


I went out to a corner of the hotel grounds (looking out for wild life) and practiced some of my linedancing to stay fresh! The ground was uneven and some of the hotel staff came out to see what I was doing and went back inside lol

After our breakfast in the hotel room we went out to see the markets of Zimbabwe where we walked past an open bakery that had previously been closed every time we walked by! I tried this chocolate tart while Mike had the lemon donut. They were ok :( 

We enjoyed walking around the markets in Zimbabwe and actually saw someone staining a finished wooden carving! The colors and patterns here are exquisite!

We had arranged for a taxi to the airport and made it early to the airport for our flight to Johannesburg. We arrived just as a massive group of pensioners (what they call seniors here) were unloading from a bus with several large suitcases each :o! We hurried through security and over to our gate as to not be delayed since we only had backpacks and didn't want to miss out on overhead compartment space. 

  


We made it to our gate but there were already more than 50 people there! With no signs or boards with flight information we patiently waited for our boarding time. It was about 40 minutes ahead of our boarding time when they began boarding the flight to Johannesburg! I got suspicious and asked the couple across from us what time their flight was. It was the flight ahead of ours so we were relieved.

  


Well, the relieved feeling didn't last long when every single senior came down to wait with us at our gate (normally I would not be bummed by this but they were the most entitled people I'd ever met!) They lined up directly in front of us - who had been waiting an hour now! Then, when it was time to board, they tried to bring up even more people in their group who didn't even have their passports or boarding passes ready and some of them were still coming from the restroom. Oh goodness what a test of patience. 

  


Thankfully, we manuevered around them while they got their bags sorted and boarded the plane with enough time to get overhead compartment space. We were ready to get to Jburg.

  


Now you may or may not be familiar with Johannesburg but we were cautioned of the dangers in the city so we were nervous but hoped that our pocket of the city we were staying in and using ubers as the only transportation would keep us safe.
