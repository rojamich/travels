Some things we wish we had done/known for Rome.

I would not have spent any more time in Rome. I’m so grateful we saw everything we wanted in one trip! But I much prefer the smaller cities of Italy over the hustle and bustle of Rome. 

The driving in Rome is generally orderly but the roads do not have lines for the most part. Cars do not like stopping for pedestrians and will honk if other people stop. If you want to get across the crosswalk and there’s no sign, you have to start confidently walking and pray they stop for you. I would not recommend biking or scootering here as its all cobblestone. 

The COVID restrictions were strict here and we almost didn’t make it into the country. FFP2 masks are required here with no deviation. You had to be fully vaccinated to travel here. They will check your COVID documentation everywhere you go.

Both the LABARA and EE sim cards worked here efficiently. 

City Mapper was ideal for Rome and helped us determine the best way to get around the city. However, Google Maps was more useful for walking routes though and City Mapper didn’t quite have that figured out. 

The metros and bus were inefficient, outdated, and overfilled. 

The water in the restaurants costs to drink, it does not come with your meal for free but there are fountains all over the city where you can fill your bottles up-straight from the aqueduct! The bathrooms cost to use so have your coins ready! 

Mostly Italian and English speakers here. We could hear some German and French at times though!

Days visited: February 21st \- 26th

Daily weather: High 40s in the morning and up to 70 degrees during the day!

Total distance walked: 47.34 miles

Transportations used: metro, bus, train, uber, and walking.
