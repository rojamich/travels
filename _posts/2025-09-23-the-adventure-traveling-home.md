The lodge boarded us up on the riverboat to bring us back to Iquitos. We were all loaded up and about halfway down the river when a girl realized she had forgotten a bag. We turned around, and thankfully, a lodge employee met us with the bag, so we didn't have to go all the way back!

After an hour boat ride, we were bused back to Iquitos, which took about 1.5 hours. They dropped us at the airport at 3pm at our request (rather than wait in their office for hours) because we didn't want to have to pay for a taxi later. The airport was actually pretty comfortable, and the food wasn't bad! We even had wifi, where I was able to work on the blog, and we could check out the fantasy football happenings!

A massive thunderstorm rolled through while we waited, and it was absolutely delightful!! I loved watching it and was surrounded by the relaxing nature of it. Each time the sliding doors open, we watched the rain pour in sideways and saw the travelers rush in, drenched, with rain-soaked luggage.

At the Lima airport, I found us these snacks! They taste like strawberries, and I got a chocolate one as well. So good!

Our flight finally boarded at 930pm to Lima. It was an easy 1.5-hour flight, and we finished our book, Taipan!

*SPOILER WARNING*

 It was a sad ending :(

At Lima, we had to rush like hell to get to our next flight and made it as they were boarding! It was a 4.5-hour flight from Lima to El Salvador. We both passed out and slept pretty hard, thankfully! When we landed, there was a line of airport security officers standing at our gate with a police dog. Just checking passengers via scent.

We slept through the flight and woke up at roughly 5am, landing in El Salvador, and searched for the lounge so we could grab breakfast and water before our next flight to San Francisco. The food wasn't bad! We filled up, played our NYT games, and headed to our flight. 

At the gate, they had security, so we had to put our backpacks through and walk through the metal detectors. It was kind of odd!

We had a spacious exit row to ourselves for the 5.5-hour flight and settled in reading and drinking coffee. About 2.5 hours into the flight, the stewardess looked out the plane windows towards the wing of the plane that a passenger was pointing at. About 30 minutes later, the pilot advised that we would be turning back for El Salvador. There was a non-urgent fire in the wing, and we would have to get another flight. 

Mike and I have never had a flight turned around before this trip. And now it had happened twice??? What in the world! We endured the entire flight back and then sat at the gate waiting for more information. They quickly had a new plane lined up for us, and we would depart in about an hour. We grabbed food and snacks and had to go back through the security at the gate. They handed out meal vouchers and compensation for the flight delay. 

We were back on our way, same seats, new plane, and the flight went by quickly. I finished another book that was super good and made me miss Africa (Far Horizons by Tony Park).

Happy to finally land in SFO, we scarfed down Carne Asada fries and grabbed a coffee. We were able to watch the TNF game at our gate before takeoff. There were no issues on our 1.5-hour flight, thankfully! And we made it home about 1am on Friday morning (supposed to be about 7pm, landing in PDX resulted in 1030pm).

To recap it was:

Iquitos > Lima 1.5 hrs

Lima > El Salvador 4.5 hrs

El Salvador > SFO 6.5 hrs first attempt

El Salvador > SFO 5.5 hrs second attempt

SFO > PDX 1.5 hrs

Did I mention we were covered in bug bites during this journey? Starting the Aguas Caliente, I started getting bitten, and Mike was bitten starting in the Amazon. We treated our clothes, which worked really well! But they got us at night! There was a little gap between the door and frame that they snuck in through. In total, Mike had 55 bites at the end, and I had 131. OW!
