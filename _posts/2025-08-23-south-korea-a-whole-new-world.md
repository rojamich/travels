To preface this blog, I was pretty nervous and unsure of what to expect in South Korea! We planned this trip around our dream of seeing Japan. Well, since we were in the area, why not stop by South Korea? When will we be this close again? So we tacked on 5 more days to see as much of the country as possible! Mike was stationed there during his Military service and had always talked about how amazing the country was. He got to spend a little time in Seoul and he loved it! His adventure really inspired this trip :)

Since we are on the topic of uncertainty around touring South Korea, I wasn't alone. We had a few family members confused on why we wanted to travel there, and didn't care to hear about what it had to offer. I also had Facebook friends proclaiming how dangerous it was there. 

**Now** I know it severely rivals Japan in what it has to offer, in completely different ways! I loved our time there, and I can't wait to tell you all about it:) I'm sorry it's taken me so long; life got in the way.

When we researched our late arrival from Japan to South Korea, we needed to determine the best way to get from the airport to our hotel, which was about an hour's drive away. It would probably be too late for the train, a taxi-limo was expensive, so Uber it was! How cool is this Uber? A very friendly man jumped out and loaded us and our bags into the vehicle. Thankfully, we had practiced our hellos and thank yous in Korean on our way over.

It was pretty late on our way in, but we took time to appreciate how cool their crosswalks are! Notice the color green or red on the curb to indicate the direction of walking, and that the number countdown is green! This is also a beautiful view of the cityscape from the highway.

We checked into our hotel room close to 1230am and were grateful for a comfortable stay near the public transportation and the heart of the uptown area. This made it easy to walk and find food, which, as you know, is very important lol.

One block away was a beautiful shop that sold tarts - I swear this country knew the way to my heart: food. I promised the tarts I would come back. We didn't fully understand how the cards worked, so we purchased them but didn't know we had to add money. The way we discovered this was when we tried to board the bus, and the tourists in front of us made the same mistake! We all disembarked and returned to the store to load the cards. Take two went much better, and we were off trying a new country's transportation! It was peaceful but fast! These bus drivers don't mess around.
