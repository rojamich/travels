A brisk and beautiful morning greeted us in Bergen! We woke up and promptly fell back to sleep, giving us 9 hours of sleep, yay!

We made breakfast at home and set out for a cafe to work in. We were able to walk by the old town wharf that Bergen is known for

The houses are so cute and colorful! The fish market is right on the wharf and well-stocked with fresh seafood options. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782922238/20260630_113153_zwcf5g.jpg)

The narrow alleyways wove through the oldtown, giving me Harry Potter, Diagon Alley vibes!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1782922312%2F20260630_141855_armwkd.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1782922315%2F20260630_142043_edyvbu.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

The building directly next to our cafe choice had a troll living inside it!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782922378/20260630_141233_h27f9s.jpg "Can you spot him?")

The cafe owners were so sweet and happy! We ordered two coffees and one waffle with Nutella!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782922430/20260630_115005_lidg9l.jpg)

It was all delectable! We got plenty of blogging/work done and set out to see a bit of the town before lunch.

Look at this amazing architecture! How cute is that??

Right on the waterline, you can see the Rosenkrantz tower.

Between the tower and St. Mary's Church, this cityscape is quite a sight to behold

This showstopping alleyway led us to the famous Trekroneren, a stand that has been serving hotdogs since 1978. But not just any hotdogs, we got Reindeer dogs! 

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1782922836%2F20260630_145435_x2aq3v.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1782922842%2F20260630_150700_ejhb3x.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1782922839%2F20260630_150122_ri2h7s.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22We%20bought%20one%20first%2C%20in%20case%20I%20didn't%20like%20it.%20I%20have%20historically%20not%20liked%20hotdogs.%20Then%20I%20went%20back%20up%20and%20got%20my%20own!%22%7D %}

It came with spicy mustard, lingonberry sauce, and fried onions - YUM! First time with lingonberries too, very good. 

Norway was set to play their knockout game tonight against the Ivory Coast for the World Cup. The energy here was high and many spots were offering the game. We have been just watching highlights online but we thought, what the heck, lets watch the game with the locals!

After some research, there was a tent offering the game in the middle of the city. It was 3pm and the game was at 7pm so we headed straight there to see if there was a line for tickets. Sadly, they were sold out. They directed us to the tent near the bus station as they were going to have drop ins available. We gave our thanks and rushed to the bus station. An attendant directed us down the street - harried, we kept on! 

Suddenly, people were all around us, rushing across busy streets and fast walking towards the tent. It felt just like being in San Francisco for a Giants game! Here is what the back of the line looked like!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782923128/20260630_154214_skuk4k.jpg)

Yep, couldn't see the entrance and it wrapped around two corners! Mike got in line as I scurried to the front to find out what the deal was. 

> Me to the gentleman first in line: ...Is this the line for drop ins??
>
> Man: Yes! Can you believe it?!
>
> Me: Oh no, ok well. Bummer. Have you seen the line? It wraps around the square!
>
> Man: I hear they're giving out 600 tickets so it should be fine!
>
> Me: Oh seriously? Wow!

I rushed back to Mike with the news and we decided to stick it out to see what happens! It was a bit warm but we were undeterred! Security came by every once in awhile to scoot everyone forward and the Norwegians behind us translated what instructions he gave 

At about 410pm the line began moving quite a bit and we gathered hope! Surely security would send people home if there were too many of us, right?!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782923481/20260630_161148_a32kqy.jpg)

We killed time by trying to guess how much they would be charging per ticket. Mike said $10 and I guessed $50. Almost to the front they were checking IDs so we hurried to have those out along with our tap pay. Turns out, we didn't need either! Too old to be carded and the entry was FREE! CAN YOU BELIEVE THAT?? We found a table in the center with a great view!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782923610/20260630_162928_rangsf.jpg)

Mike headed to get us drinks and I made friends with our table mates!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782923652/20260630_165137_pecgt6.jpg)

Mike came returned and said, where did you get face paint??? By then, more people had packed into our table and soon, we were surrounded by the most welcoming Norwegians you could hope to meet! We had been sold a pack of lies. Norwegians have been portrayed as cold, introverts who want nothing to do with...well...anyone! This hasn't touched our actual impression/experience!

The venue put on a "Kahoots" game to quiz people on fotball (how you spell football in Norway). Our new friends translated for us and Mike had fun guessing random answers lol.

Around 6pm we started getting hungry so we decided to try some nachos, Norway style. They are made with doritos?? It was actually really good and was filled with chili at the bottom!
