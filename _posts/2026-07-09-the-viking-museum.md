An easy morning for us meant straight to the breakfast buffet downstairs. Fully stocked with eggs, bacon, fresh sourdough (still warm!), yogurts, coffees, juices, fruits & veggies, and desserts (for some reason).

It was a good and fortifying breakfast. I really did not want to leave this hotel. I was so cozy and relaxed, plus it was raining while I was reading! We had a late morning check-out so I took a nap while Mike played his steam deck. 

We finally departed and set off on foot for the nearest cafe (a 6 minute walk). We secured seats and quickly realized this was not a working-on-your-laptop cafe. This was not a cozy cafe. This was not a relaxing cafe. It was make-as-much-money-as-quickly-as-possible cafe. Unfortunately, we had already ordered from the grumpy cashier and decided this was a good learning experience.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783801174/20260710_111257_phixn1.jpg)

My pastry wasn't bad! But everything else was. I can't keep trying iced coffees. It doesn't get hot enough for them to make good iced coffee here lol! We read for a little bit and then hit the road.

Our only big stop of the day was the Viking Museum! Since we missed out on the one in Oslo (repairs), we decided to pay this one a visit.  Thankfully, there was an English tour starting shortly so we huddled up at the meeting point. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783800686/20260710_125429_vkx5rv.jpg "How cute is this exit sign?!")

The guide quickly appeared and ushered us all to a huge room, three rooms away from the main entryway. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783801390/20260710_130811_y3jqen.jpg)

We were all seated on benches while she educated us about the Nordic Viking history. It was really fascinating and fun to learn - especially from her Italian accent!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783801804/20260710_131503_r1o1qm.jpg "Here she is explaining the prep for stockfish! We saw some hanging earlier in town that day!")

Something really interesting was how they used fungus and urine to start and maintain fires that traveled with them! "They would collect a fungus called touchwood from tree bark and boil it for several days in urine before pounding it into something akin to felt. The sodium nitrate found in urine would allow the material to smolder rather than burn, so Vikings could take fire with them on the go." 

Another fun fact was that they were surprisingly hygienic and bathed frequently for that time. 

One that surprised me but shouldn't have was that they didn't wear horned helmets. Can you imagine how impractical and heavy those would be? I didn't even think about it! Damn you Minnesota Vikings!

Last one I'll share is the history of the Bluetooth symbol. The Vikings used a symbolic language (rather than alphabetic). 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783801726/20260710_132943_szkc74.jpg "Any symbols looking familiar yet??")

"The Bluetooth logo is a bind rune merging the Younger Futhark runes (Hagall) (ᚼ) and (Bjarkan) (ᛒ), Harald’s initials." This quote is actually from the [Bluetooth website](https://www.bluetooth.com/about-us/bluetooth-origin/)!

After the tour we were given time to explore the exhibitions and down by the lake they had axe throwing, archery, and a ride on a viking ship!

I was especially thrilled to feel like Thor for a moment!

[Source with many fun facts about Vikings](https://www.history.com/articles/10-things-you-may-not-know-about-the-vikings)!

I was getting hangry so we headed to our next town: Leknes, Norway! A town with about 3800 residents. We found a restaurant and I ordered the fish & chips while Mike got the Stockfish - from what we learned about that day!

Both were fine. As far as food goes, this wasn't my favorite day. No good desserts in sight. All that hiking and no ice cream or gelato?? What's a girl to do!? No wonder Norwegians are so fit.

We headed to our Airbnb and it is *very* nice. Tons of amenities and very roomy! One much-needed nap later and some reading, we headed for dinner. We had no idea this night was so popular here and the sushi place was slammed! They said 30 minute wait. We said no problem and they actually seated us quickly but let us know our food would take awhile. No worries! We were just happy we could get in (especially in a town this small).

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783802393/20260710_191235_q0lkqh.jpg)

The sushi and gyoza were good and I'm so relieved I like more fish food now. I think I would've starved to death or eaten only cheese burgers here if not. 

Our last stop was the big grocery store in town to shop for breakfast! They had a sliding walkway to get in??

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783802476/20260710_162706_qes13z.jpg)

The grocery store was in the lower floor of the mall 😮 This is where I saw something interesting.
