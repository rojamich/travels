Wow! What a crazy adventure on our first trip to South America! Getting to see the city, food, and culture of Lima, the beauty of Cusco, the mind-blowing site of Machu Picchu, loving each train ride, and exploring the depths of the Amazon was just non-stop new and exciting experiences!

While we loved the trip, the interactions were challenging. Most were not as welcoming as other countries we have visited, and our eagerness to speak Spanish was often met with neutrality or impatience. The poverty in some areas was hard to witness and understand that we couldn't help everyone, and we were lucky to be born in privilege.

Nevertheless, we are so grateful we got to go and have no regrets. We had all our vaccines prior to departure and took daily medicine to avoid Malaria. Thankfully, we stayed healthy the entire 2.5-week trip!

Mike used the eSIM app here, which worked well everywhere we went! Wifi was also available in most restaurants, and was only unavailable to us in the Amazon.

We utilized planes, trains, Uber, walking, paddleboards, a canoe, and boats for our modes of transportation here!

As for walking...not nearly as much as in other countries!

Lima > 30.12 miles

Cusco > 23.08 miles

MP > 27.88 miles

The Amazon > 13.17 miles

Total Miles Walked in 2.5weeks is 94.25 miles!

That's like walking from Atlanta to Columbus, GA!

OR

Portland, OR to Seaside, OR!

OR 

Sacramento, CA to South Lake Tahoe, CA!

Thanks so much for coming on this journey with us :) 

See you in December for Vietnam!
