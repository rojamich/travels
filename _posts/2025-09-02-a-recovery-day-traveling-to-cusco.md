Many countries have bag size limits (even for smallish carry-ons like backpacks). We have made it through every location so far without being flagged for oversized bags (we each only have one backpack) but this day they were enforcing the rules pretty strictly and lots of passengers ended up with a fee. It was a shruggable hang up since we had gotten away with it for so long.

The flight was an easy 1.5 hours and we were flying though the mountains of Cusco! It was really intimidating at one point because it was towards the end of our flight and we were climbing, climbing, climbing, and suddenly came over the ridgeline and damn near straight onto the runway - that's how high Cusco is! We are at 11,150 feet elevation here - for reference, Corvallis, Oregon is at 220 elevation. When we exited the aircraft, it felt like someone was giving me a real tight hug. Apparently, altitude sickness is very common here (Headache, Nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, fatigue, trouble sleeping, dizziness/lightheadedness, vision changes) but thankfully neither of us have gotten sick.

You may have noticed a very familiar and colorful flag in their main square downtown! This is not an LGBTQ+ flag, it is a celebration of the Inka Culture. Save yourself the confusion lol. 

  


There are way more street venders here that are trying to peddle sunglasses, tours, massages, trinkets, etc. In the main square, you couldn't go a few feet (no exaggeration) without being prompted to purchase something. After a bit of research, we took one vender up on a couples massage (advertised at 20 soles for a half hour for each). We were skeptical but went to the massage parlor which ended up just being a big room that separated massage tables with a sheet for a wall. We disrobed and were _then_ given a little pamplet that advised that was the price for a relaxing massage with little to no pressure. I am a firm pressure gal where Mike prefers medium. This would've cost us 80 soles for Mike and 100 for me. We decided to try our luck elsewhere.

That night, we ventured out to a local Indian food restaurant (my favorite cuisine) and the food was good, not fantastic, but I was really happy to have a hot meal with good spices. They hadn't mixed in the heat as it was decor on top of the tikka, so when I took a bite, I was like, hm not very hot. At the same time, Mike took a bite and said HOLY cow that's hot! We quickly realized what had happened XD 

  


Here is the lovely, lit up walk back to our hotel. Our room is great and its nice a cold here at night (40s) so we were able to sleep with the balcony open. Unfortunately, there are quite a few nightclubs that blast loud music (how old am I lol) until 330am. You can also hear each coming and going in the inn. Including the tours that start in the wee hours and have pick ups at our inn. Each roller suitcase that went by was documented. While I didn't get much sleep, I have read four books since departing from PDX!
