We arrived in Brussels and left the train station only to be immersed in the sketchiest area of the city. (If you visit, definitely arrive in Brussels – Nord, NOT Brussels – Midi-Zuid. There was graffiti everywhere, gentrification, and some of the angriest looking people you’ll see there. Thankfully, once you get closer to the city center, the buildings are ornate and the people are extremely friendly. 

Everyone was really understanding of us not knowing the language and helped us with our pronunciation and finding exactly what we needed. Something we were not prepared for was that Brussels has a largely French-speaking population and Bruges (our next city) has a largely Dutch-speaking population.

Our first true stop here had to be a chocolate shop which, essentially, has now ruined me for all other chocolate in life. It was so rich and dark and flavorful my whole life is changed lol. I must have more. Why does that shop not deliver to the US??

Michael popped his first piece into his mouth and said, “This is what heaven tastes like”!!! A high compliment I will NEVER forget! It was a milk chocolate Belgian truffle with cocoa powder coating.

As you walk down the streets of Brussels, the wafting smell of waffles surrounds you. I am not kidding. I could not turn the corner without the alluring aroma of fresh, hot waffles seducing me. 

The first statue on our list was the dog peeing, aka Het Zinneke, it was so cute!
