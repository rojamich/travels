We got to the station to take our planned train into Frankfurt from Bad Homburg. We quickly found out tht there were several delays and cancelled trains due to repairs and ours was rescheduled for 3 hours later! We had bought a day pass train ticket so we thought ALL fares to Frankfurt were cancelled, so we went to ask about our options at the info desk. The man who helped us looked puzzeled and said...well why don't you just take the next train? It leaves for Frankfurt every 30 minutes? Oh WOW! HA! We had no idea. 10 minutes later, our train had arrived and we were on our way!

After a quick coffee stop, we walked through the financial district, a Michael Rojas favorite <3

Every building was completely different from the next on every street! They were so unique we felt like we could walk around for hours and not see the same type twice.

It was really cool to see the way the gold locks glinted in the sunlight! We tried to come back for another photo later without the ferries but they were parked and it became overcast. 

Fun Fact: did you know that Frankfurt is Germany's most diverse city?

  


Mike found a very odd sight for us to visit via a hike! It was only a 30 minute ride on the train away from Frankfurt so we went for it! On the way, a woman spoke to us in German asking if the train was going the way she needed and she felt she may be lost! Mike understood way more than I did but we didn't know the route past our stop. Fortunately, she mentioned a specific stop and at the same moment I looked at the screen that was scrolling through all of the possible stops! She was faced away from it so she couldn't see it until I pointed it out and asked (in German), is that your stop? She was so relieved! And never figured out that I only speak ein bisschen deutsch, haha!

The inscription next to the tree says, "I have been peed on for 300 years. Today I pee back!" As you can see, there is a spout in it that water shoots out of! It was quite a sight to see! We walked around the lake and watched the dogs sprint into the water having a blast :)

The train brought us back to Frankfurt where we got amazing massages and a woman bent me into a pretzel, YEE-OW!! We feel fantastic now though! 

The time finally came. the Moment we were waiting for...KHINKALIIII! A Georgian Restaurant in Frankfurt was open at 6pm and we raced over. Finding the entrance was a bit more challenging but after several rounds of stairs, we finally nailed it. It was really fancy and we were terribly underdressed. Thankfully they didn't care and we got our food ordered. It was fantastic food and I wish we were going back for more!
