The chefs were amazingly patient, funny, and full of food knowledge. They taught us about the history of sushi in Japan and we learned there are more than 5,000 sushi restaurants in all of Japan and only 2,000 Starbucks! They taught us little techniques to remember how to perfect our rolls at home and we even learned you can cut the rolls with scissors instead of a knife! The rice gets VERY sticky so it's important to wet your hands and tools between use.

We also learned how to make nigiri, which Mike ended up enjoying despite my adding too much wasabi! I tried a salmon roll (no thank you) but am not ready for nigiri - one day! I still prefer Americanized sushi (maki sushi) and fried is, of course, the most delicious.
