Possessed with excitement, we hurried through breakfast and headed downstairs to wait with the family for deboarding.

We all made it out and towards the taxistand to load them up for the journey to the airport. We managed to explain to the taxi driver that we would not be joining them and hugged everyone goodbye. I don't think Mario wanted to let go. Mike played games with him every chance they had onboard and I know they're going to miss it.    
  
Mike and I began the walk to the metro and it was already getting warm! We were grateful it was not in the 100s as it had been two weeks prior! About 10 minutes away from the cruise port, a taxi driver stood in our path of travel and offered us a ride, we declined, wanting to walk, and he informed us "the metro is not working". We replied that was ok, we could walk anyways. Does that really work for him??   
  
We arrived at the metro, which was indeed working, and boarded up for our ride into the Athens city center. We were glad to be one bagging it this ride.   
It was a short walk to our hotel (the Moxy- Athens) and we were greeted by two large dog statues!

It feels like a lawless country, not from a safety perspective but from the stark contrast of coming from Germany where jaywalking will get you an earful from the nearest Oma (grandma).  
An example would be that you have to walk across the street with confidence or cars will never stop. Or that theres no real lines when waiting for things. Pushing, holding out children ahead of you to gain a spot, and cutting are the only real ways to get anything. Again, nothing dangerous, just SO different from Germany.    
  
Our next stop would be directly in the middle of the busy shopping area, a medieval, Eastern Orthodox church sits, looking very out of place!

We made it to a local gyro shop near our hotel and I didn't remember to take photos- oops! But I asked the originally frowny guy at the counter how to say please in Greek. (Everyone else in the shop was super friendly). He immediately brightened and thought for a moment. He responded with para-ka-low (spelled parakaloume). Its really fun to say! And he was very kind after that!   
The host asked us if we wanted to sit front or on the side of the building and we said on the side, I added parakaloume and he did a triple take back at me! He said, "Are you Greek?!"   
We laughed and Mike said no, but in her heart she is. He loved it! He seated us and we said Eftharisto and he ate it up. We were thrilled that he was so happy! The gyros came out and were good! Not as seasoned as Doner's in Germany but they had less veggies (which, of course, I liked) and a really good sauce that had a hint of sour cream to me. The ones in Mykonos were the best thus far!
