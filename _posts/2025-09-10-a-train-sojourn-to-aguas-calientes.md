We woke up after a wonderful night of sleep, to the brisk air of Ollantatambo. The smell of nature, combined with a scent of campfire and cold air made me feel like we were camping.

Our host made us all eggs, toast, and served orange juice. His family lived there with him so we got to see the whole family in action! After some reading and packing up, we headed to  a local cafe for a light lunch before taking the train to Aguas Calientes!

After showing our tickets and passports for the third time, we boarded the beautiful train which offered large windows and comfy seats! The train was PACKED and we were nearly knee to knee with the couple in front of us!

  


  


They offered an Incan play which seemed similar to the story of Romeo and Juliet! Mike has been humming the song from it for four days 🤣 We listened to our audiobook during the 1.5 hour train ride and were so relaxed because it started raining! How beautiful to watch it stream down the windows as we wove through the Andean Mountains 😍

The train spat us out in an awesome market! Our hotel was at the very top of the hill in Aguas Calientes which gave us some great views! We had the choice to take the elevator (which was taking awhile) or the stairs. That elevator looked as reliable as the ancient ones in Italy so we opted for the stairs. Here, and in no other hotel we stayed in, the first floor doesn't count as one. So we walked four floors of stairs to get to our room on the third floor!
