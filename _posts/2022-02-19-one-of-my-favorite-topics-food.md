There was a restaurant next door to the cafe, called, “La Bucherie” which had the nicest employees you will ever find! They also had the best paprika French fries lol. 

I had the “American burger” which was essentially a beef patty with house sauce and American cheese on a baguette. I figured that was close enough to count as French food but familiar enough to be comfortable. Mike went all the way trying new stuff with a Steak that was seared on the outside but raw on the inside and I can’t remember what that’s called. As soon as we arrived at the restaurant, they changed the music from Middle Eastern to American haha! The guy who served us asked us if we wanted any coffee or anything else at the end and when we said no, he said, Ah! Do you want the sleeps?! Yes, yessir. We want the sleeps lol.

Now, I haven’t gone too far in-depth on the pastries there. We stuck to macarons mostly for the first couple days because there weren’t too many other options near us (with one exception). That changed the next day. Overall, in Paris, we consumed an obscene number of pastries lol.

We ended the day at Café de Tullaries next to the hotel. Michael ordered a Crème Brulee and I had the crepe. I tried his dessert and was coming up short for a way to describe it, until it hit me! A stinky fart! That’s what it tasted like! Mike was not expecting this descriptor and burst out laughing. I believe this is the hardest I have ever made him laugh. I tried to capture the moment and it came out blurry but you get it!
