Per tradition, our late night flight did not leave on time (the curse of the late flight delays!) Instead of arriving in Riga, Latvia (the largest city in the baltics) for a short layover at 1120pm, we arrived at 1150pm and our next flight was AT 1150pm - UGH! We raced to our connection and SURPRISE! We had to go through passport control even though we were just passing through the country for about 30 minutes in the airport. There were only two people working passport control but we were fast enough walkers that we made it there first and met an unsmiling man who seemed mildly amused by our excitement. 

We spent roughly 1 minute there and continued on to our flight that was just starting to board (WHEW) No problems fitting bags aboard so we were relieved we haven't had any issues yet!

We were *SUPPOSED* to sleep on these flights but found it impossible because they never turned off the lights! Its an overnight flight people! They also closed the rear bathrooms so we had one bathroom for a fullish flight...

Instead of sleeping, I continued reading my book ["Till Summer Do Us Part" by Meghan Quinn](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/227081370-till-summer-do-us-part) and Mike listened to his book (continuing the Red Rising series) while playing his Steam Deck. We did have a bit of excitement on the flight when we flew past a storm!

Sorry, I know its hard to see with the reflection! 

It was a short 4.5 flight and, before we knew it, the sun was rising over Georgia 🥟🌄

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784460879/20260714_043113_gze7md.jpg)

We stood in a fast-moving, long line for passport control and I excitedly said, "Gamarjoba!" She was also amused by excitement 🤣 She asked how long I would be here (Mike was sent to a separate line) and then released me. We both started towards the exit and wove our way through the taxi drivers. We were using bolt here (their Uber) as taxi drivers can be predatory sometimes. A taxi driver approached us outside as we were getting a bolt driver on the app and he said, "Ah, I am with Bolt" and checked his phone. Nice try dude.

It was about a 35 minute ride to our Airbnb with our driver who rocked to EDM/pop at 5am to stay awake 😮 Thankfully he drove well and dropped us off very close to our Airbnb (we live on a hill, yay for a daily workout!)

We have THE coolest key ever to get into our place!!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784461354/20260714_062152_rmek0u.jpg "Doesn't everyone wish they had a key like this to their house at some point??")

We set up a few things per usual (electronics & hygiene kits) showered, and slept hard for a few hours so we weren't moving through water all day. The AC here is surprisingly MAGNIFICENT!!! Like, better than the ones at home. We are so pleasantly relieved because our first day here is 33c (92f) 🥵

Our spot is up one flight of stairs and has all the necessities (great job finding this spot honey!) Our washer is excellent - spacious enough to work, sleep, cook, and do yoga!

It was time for a walk around so we explored our surroundings - we are across the street from an Italian bistro and a half block from the Italian embassy. One of our first stops took us to a cute cafe a block away. It's run by an ex-lawyer and she was so kind! She asked us about our travel here, told us about the politics, and practiced Georgian with us. We learned that continuous electricity was reinstated in Tbilisi in 2004! Wow! That is incredible to think of this thriving city as lacking electricity only 22 years ago! Mike enjoyed his Americano and we headed to find me a snack. 

We stumbled upon a cafe / bookstore and walked in to see a sweet Asian girl trying to communicate with an employee who was frustrated they were saying the same thing and not understanding each other! He was saying cold latte and she was saying iced. To be fair, I would want to distinguish 🤣

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462030/20260714_122403_ei4fla.jpg)

I got a delicious tart (one of my favorite desserts) and a terrible latte. Look at me very closely in the eyeballs, it was so bad. Watery milk bad. Looks like coffee is off the menu in Tbilisi for Jenna. We browsed the bookstore and headed back out to see more of Tbilisi. 

Its really cool that instead of many street crossings in the busy areas, they have underground tunnels with shops!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462318/20260714_130817_hrbnrt.jpg)

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462377/20260714_131018_mdxove.jpg)

Its much greener here than I remember!

We circled Freedom Square and searched for a good lunch spot. Breakfast typically takes place around 10am here (if at all), lunch between 2-3 and dinner is 8pm+. Send Jenna patience please lol.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462374/20260714_134445_snyxt6.jpg)

There was a fancy gym nearby so we decided to check it out. It was $250 a month PER PERSON! That would be an insane price for the US much less Georgia!

We found a good looking Khinkali spot (the food Jenna dreams of ever since visiting here 6 years ago) and found a spot inside. It was decorated with Spanish! 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462688/20260714_140243_og0oou.jpg)

We order our Khinkali (beef and pork dumpling with tons of herbs!), Mike tries a local beer, and we split a tarragon lemonade.

I'm so happy it's everything I remembered and Mike says it far surpasses the ones we've had in NYC 😮

The woman running the shop teaches us how to say delicious (gemrieli) in Georgian. We thanked her (madloba) and headed towards the local medium-sized grocery store to stock up.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784462935/20260714_145621_qorbdx.jpg "This is the typical sentiment here (and many places). Russian has a history of invading, controlling, encroaching Georgia.")

Many protests here are anti-Russian in nature and they have a similar relationship with Russia as Ukraine does with Russia. 

Later that evening we headed to check out the nearby gym outdoor gym - its pretty freaking awesome!

There is a resistance knob on each machine and it'll be great to use this with our month here!

That night we head to a Khinkali House nearby (one of many but the same chain Jenna visited her first time here). It was busy as all get out and the music was BLARING inside! 

We tried Adjaruli Khachapuri - a bread boat with cheese and egg!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1784463476/20260714_204520_hmlghh.jpg)

The food and lemonade were good but nowhere near as good as this afternoon's! Another sweaty walk later and we were home for a shower, yoga, and to sleep for 11.5 hours!
