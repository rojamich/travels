We caught the train to an entertainment-filled mall- complete with bowling, table tennis, karaoke, darts, billiards, a poop museum, and tons of stores! It opened just as we got there and the sliding metallic doors are not human-operated. It was kind of eery to watch them automatically open all at once around us!

  


We walked around to experience and take it all in, window shopping. After filling up on amusement, we tried their donut store which was fine. I found that I do really like strawberry-flavored foods and have decided to try a strawberry again soon. I haven't had one since I was little!

We made it to Shinjuku Omoide Yokocho which is a beautiful spot with winding, cozy alleyways, filled with mouth-watering food options. We had a hard time deciding which place to eat! We settled on a ramen spot and tried garlic-flavored ramen. It was SO garlicky I loved it lol. Mike likes to call me his garlic queen because I add garlic to all my food at home. Don't worry, I also really like mint gum 😂. 

  


We headed over to Shinjuku (the neighboring district to where we are living here in Shibuya) and somewhere we initially debated on staying when we planned the trip. We are SO grateful to have chosen the quieter, more laid-back of the two.
