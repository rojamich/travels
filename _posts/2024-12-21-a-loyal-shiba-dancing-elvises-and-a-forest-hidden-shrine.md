But first, let's talk bathrooms. This is the biggest thing that has me questioning a return to the US. It's cold at home and yet we have not progressed to heated toilet seats everywhere. 

That's right. I'm telling you the bathrooms here (EVERYWHERE) have heated toilet seats, bidets with dryers, a music option in your stall, and warm water from the bidet. Game changer. 

Ok google, how difficult is it to get duel citizenship with US/Japan?

If you're curious, here is google's answer:

"Acquiring dual citizenship between the United States and Japan is considered very difficult because Japan generally does not recognize dual nationality, meaning that to become a Japanese citizen through naturalization, you typically need to renounce any other citizenship you hold, including your US citizenship; however, US law allows its citizens to maintain dual citizenship without penalty, creating a complex situation when attempting to naturalize in Japan while keeping your US citizenship."
