After exploring the colosseum we headed across the street in search of the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Mike was so intrigued we went towards the Temple of Venus and Roma, first. However, we learned it was actually part of the Roman Forum. This turned out to be ENORMOUS! Thankfully Rick Steve had a tour because we would have had no idea what we were looking at. The signs there were fairly vague and not easily found. We sat by the entrance arch looking out over the forum, listening to its history, before embarking onto the stops it led us to.

Following the map was slightly complex because there were a lot of arches, columns, and temples that looked like basilicas and vice versa! The trouble could also be when our map was in English but the names on the signs for the items were in Italian. That brings me to a reminder. In Paris, _a lot_(if not most) people, spoke some English. Despite Rome being a huge tourist attraction, many people spoke no English. This was tricky when ordering food, at times, as things move pretty fast in Rome. I felt like I moved in slow motion compared to most! The scene was impressive as a lot of it had stood the test of time despite floods and earthquakes and pillaging!

We were there for hours! Exhaustion was the best way to describe our state after finishing the Roman Forum. But we had to keep going! The Palatine Hill was in the same complex and we couldn’t pass it up. We saw Augustus’ homes and many ancient ruins.

  


I was dead on my feet afterwards but grateful we hadn’t gone to lunch and come back because here is the line around 1pm! We were able to see the line for the colosseum on our way out as well!
