Finally a good evening's rest and we woke up feeling fabulous! An easy workout later and we are packed for the long day ahead! 

We picked out a promising breakfast spot about 40 minutes away and have really liked walking to and from all our meals here! It's also called a Verdauungsspaziergang - German for "digestion walk" (a post-meal stroll). How fun is that?!

It was a super cute and cozy cafe - I ordered fried eggs, hashbrowns, guacamole, and an iced caramel latte! Mike got a flat white, and eggs over toast :) It was all so fresh and delicious! What a great start to the day so far :) Feeling super!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781554610/20260615_080206_ocptgn.jpg "As I type this, I am conspiring how I can convince Mike to go back here tomorrow...")

We headed for the West Minster bridge to start our [Rick Steve's tour](https://www.ricksteves.com/watch-read-listen/audio/audio-tours) where he audibly walks us through the area - for free and with so much passion and excitement! We love his tours!

I saw something I didn't notice our last trip here - these walk signs!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781554505/20260615_074112_cw8miw.jpg "So different than others we have seen in other countries!")

I really enjoyed this random chicken statue as we walked down the street!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1781554515/20260615_083725_umojqn.jpg "In case you're wondering, no, they do not ship internationally. I did ask for you.")

Using my brand new headphones (heart eyes), we enjoyed listening to Rick Steve give us factoids and history about the city of London!

We learned so much on this tour! Things like:

* Big Ben actually refers to the bell, not the tower or the clock! Kinda feel like my whole life is a lie now...
* Every year, they adjust the clock. If it's running fast, they place an old penny on the pendulum - too slow and the remove a penny. I definitely thought it would be a more sophisticated mechanism...
* Big Ben and the tower escaped damage during WWII
