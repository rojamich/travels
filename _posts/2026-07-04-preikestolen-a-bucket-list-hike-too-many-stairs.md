Feeling a whole bunch better, we packed up for our early hike! I was super hungry this morning too! 

This was hanging in our room and was the inspiration for a later pose on top of Preikestolen!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783343864/20260704_220316_czte2b.jpg)

They had a buffet breakfast with every jam or jelly or spread for the waffles but, somehow, no sauce for eggs 😮

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783344086/20260705_064554_dmnxqu.jpg "We tried their famous \"brown cheese\" here and it's actually really good! Its apparently, \"a sweet, caramel-tasted cheese-like food made by boiling whey, milk, and cream\"! Just what I love a combination of, sugar and cheese!")

The staff even filled our water bottles for us! It was drizzling on and off this morning so we were nervous about the view from the top.

The name of this hike is stairs, stairs, and more stairs. Ok, we should also add stone steps into the title as well.

We started early enough that there were very few people on the trail, hooray! If there were other people, they were either significantly slower or significantly faster than us. So the way up was really peaceful despite my ongoing feud with stairs. 

We alternated on who would carry the backpack to preserve both our energy and help us get in hiking shape for future challenging hikes. 

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783344496%2F20260705_081350_u85s1t.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783344497%2F20260705_083327_he1f5x.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783344500%2F20260705_075418_lfcvhq.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

As we reached the last stretch of the hike, it got a bit narrow, windy, and scary!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783345236/20260705_083459_ctfvzn.jpg)

Mike, once again, got close to the edge and I had to ask myself if he's really been afraid of heights all these years or was faking??

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783345328/20260705_083939_tddxgs.jpg)

The pulpit was magnificent and even bigger/higher than I thought it would be! Truly a staggering sight to be hold! There weren't too many people and everyone was respectful of others' photos. Those who were clueless were quickly put in check!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783345414/20260705_084423_gng0mr.jpg)

Mike headed to get in line for a photo while I searched for someone to take it for us. No one stood out so I ended up taking his photos first. I think they turned out pretty cool!

He grabbed a couple shots of the world-altering view !

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346021%2F20260705_085031_butigu.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346023%2F20260705_085027_lb1gsa.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

We swapped spots and the sun bathed me in warmth!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346090%2F20260705_085238_hxwvzz.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346105%2F20260705_085244_txtdmh.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

We found someone and scurried to get back on the rock - its so high up here! Not as scary as the boulder yesterday but still. 

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346336%2F20260705_085806_mqkx3c.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346341%2F20260705_085758_pjoumx.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22%22%7D %}

This is where we got silly and decided to pose like the poster this morning!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783346409/20260705_085822_ppyn9i.jpg "Can you blame us? It's really high up there and I don't want to get blown off the edge!")

We marched back down the mountain - it was not alllll down, unfortunately. Sometimes there were, again, stair cases. We hopped and climbed boulders and skipped over streams and tried not to slide over slick rocks.

It was close to 930 and we were grateful to have started early again. We had looked up the schedule for the hike and buses usually started arriving between 830 and 10am so we saw A LOT of crowds on their way up. Apparently, you usually don't take turns on the pulpit. You just deal with the crowds in your photos. I will forever be adamant that waking up early is the way to go! We did experience mosquitos though. I have three bites on my neck, one on my cheek, several on my scalp and another on my forehead! How?? Its so cold!

After a snack at the cafe at the bottom of the hike, we hit the road for a short 40 minute drive back to Stavanger. Here's that cool underwater tunnel!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346753%2F20260705_120757_dbb1dh.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783346750%2F20260705_120737_z77bii.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22It%20was%20a%20different%20color%20yesteday!%22%7D %}

We were able to drop our bags at the hotel to head out for some ice cream!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783346793/20260705_125658_qlt8mv.jpg)

There was a little girl in line ahead of us and I asked her if she could read me the flavors in English because I couldn't read Norwegian. She looked so excited and took the assignment *VERY* seriously. 

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783346845/20260705_130412_hjcawn.jpg)

I tried an Oreo flavor and Mike had soft-serve. We both really enjoyed the smooth and rich ice cream with many toppings!

We dropped off our rental to Hertz and caught the bus back to our hotel. It was finally nap time and we rested up so we could watch the next Norway game (vs Brazil) until midnight! It was a **DOOZY** of a game with Norway winning again! The streets went wild this evening and people were out celebrating until 530 the next morning!

Our hotel packed me a to go breakfast because I am dysfunctional without food and it was very filling and somewhat good! Another ham/cheese sandwich, yogurt, a banana, pear, and ice-cold water.

We caught the bus to our ferry the next morning and people were *still* celebrating the win! Who can stay up that late??

The 5-hour ferry was a much better experience this time. Super quiet, we had recliner seats - it was really cold in there though! We each filled up on coffee (Mike) and tea or hot water (me) to stay warm. We alternated between reading and napping - thankfully! 

Back in Bergen, we got to our hotel and started working at our favorite B[ergen cafe](https://share.google/5dPcb3l0FN8dZDuFo). I was SOOO hungry!

Tomorrow is our flight to the Lofoten Islands and I could not be more STOKED! However, I am also not excited about this adventure coming to an end. Norway has blown me away with it's beauty and I am not ready to leave!
