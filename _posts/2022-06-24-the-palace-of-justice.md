We found a chocolate place while walking around (I was still searching for a passable knock-off of our Belgian truffles) and we found an intimate store filled with chocolate but a woman who spoke ZERO English! Mike did so well explaining to her what chocolates we wanted and how many. She asked where we were from (Mike had just studied this expression and was able to recognize it!) It was really awesome to see him being able to communicate in the language he worked so hard to study to get there. As we wrapped up our purchase, she told him he spoke German beautifully and my heart swelled with pride!

  


We ended a wonderful day of sightseeing with a lovely view of the lit-up castle from our AirBnB and doing some laundry.

It was still pretty cold here but not as cold as in Munich! Notice we are still wearing our heavy Winter clothes while walking around. 

  


Our first stop the next chilly morning was the Palace of Justice in Nuremburg. This was the actual location of where Nazi leaders were tried and convicted of their war crimes. This courtroom is still used today but was not in use when we visited, so we were allowed to enter. We were even provided a free audio guide which was incredibly helpful in explaining to us the trials that occurred in that room and what was in each room of the upstairs exhibition. We thought the Palace would just be that courtroom but the upstairs exhibition was really detailed regarding the events that led up to the trials, who was on trial for which crime, evidence provided, lawyers for each defendant and the state, and we ended up hours there unexpectedly. This stop was a definite must-see, in our opinion.
