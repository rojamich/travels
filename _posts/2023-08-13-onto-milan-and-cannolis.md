We were nervous again, arriving for our flight because Europe is said to be particularly picky about bag size when bringing a carry-on and both of ours were nowhere near that small.    
  
As we were waiting to board, we were all sitting in a blocked-off area from the general public. A flight attendant walked around inspecting bags and checking tickets. If you upgrade, you can have a normal-size bag on the plane, otherwise,  it needs to be a tiny purse size. He checked the group to our left and we were sweating! He looked past us and continued onto the group to our right! Sweet relief! As long as we didn't get stopped boarding, we were clear, and no extra fees!   
  
We made it on! Two flights we should have been charged and didn't. Hallelujah!

We flipped the ac on to get it cooled down- top floor heat. The apartment was cute and cozy. We rested a bit before heading out in search of dinner.   
There was a key to get out of the inner gate of the apartment (how is this not a fire hazard??) and then there was another set of massive doors with a tiny door inside of it that we had come through to get inside. We tried every key we had and nothing worked. Our host had shown us how to get in but not out lol.  
There was a HUGE red button on the wall beside the door but I've learned not to press those anymore (not a joke). Three guys approached from behind us and we awkwardly laughed and motioned for them to go first. One of them laughed quite a bit and explained they had just done the same thing the day before but he decided to try the big red button. He said, what is the worst that could happen lol. They came at just the right time.
