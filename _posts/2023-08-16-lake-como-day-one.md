There was no shortage of excitement this morning as we eagerly got ready for a day of travel to Lake Como. We are still managing to keep everything to two backpacks, purchasing the minimum amount of souvenirs (and the smallest ones at that!)

We had our typical sugar and caffeine to hold us over until lunch and set off for the train station. We were anticipating the travel to be complicated but thankfully it was pretty straightforward (thanks to Mike being so thorough with his research)! We were also grateful to be doing A LOT of train travel moving forward - YES!! LOVE IT!

It would be just under 2 hours of train rides from Milan to Lake Como and we were determined to soak up every minute of it. This meant searching for the perfect seats- thankfully each train was fairly empty! Forward-facing seats, near a sign that listed the stops, with a full window view are the "requirements" aka hopefuls. Even better if we get a table or a set of chairs facing us to keep our bags in sight.

They look really nice at night as well.

Leading us to the garden, she turned the corner and began shushing. Is that a dog? Please let there be a dog. I have been dogless for far too long on this trip. Two of the MOST ADORABLE dashaunds were just around the corner and ready to greet us! I was over the moon!

We had just hoped to drop our bag but this was fabulous! Greeted with coffee, dogs, and a view?! I'm not sure Mike will be able to drag me away from this heaven. I was able to love on the puppies for a while until the room was ready. Mike took his shot of espresso and she led us up. We are, of course, at the top floor again (4th) Stairs - Indiana Jones Impression- why did it have to be stairs?

She showed us where we could finally do laundry- after two weeks of sink laundry - and talked about her trip to Canada. She said they were astounded at how early they ate in Banff. It was, as she explained, because of bears. Apparently, while she was there, they tried to eat at their normal 9pm but a bear came sniffing around. I could barely contain my laughter that she thought we eat at 5pm because we want to avoid bears. I will be saying that to Mike to explain my hunger from now on. Simply. Bears. 

When she showed us the coffee maker in the room she showed us the two settings, the first was one shot of espresso. The second is a "long black" aka two shots of espresso with some water on top. She excitedly said, American right?? Italy has the viewpoint that this is the same as drip coffee. We have heard this comparison every time we get coffee.

She off-handedly mentioned its an easy day trip from Lake Como to Switzerland...WHAT. Mike and I looked at each other and saw the wheels turning. Yes. Yes, that is happening now. 

  


We rested a bit, did laundry, and researched the best, easiest, and cheapest ways to get to Switzerland and what to do there. Then, we began to explore! Today was SUPPOSED to be a down day but did we listen? No. 

  


We headed to Lierna's "famous" beach and got lunch and prepared to lay out and read. We sat on a park bench at the "beach" and ate our pizza. It would have been relaxing with fewer kids and smoking.
