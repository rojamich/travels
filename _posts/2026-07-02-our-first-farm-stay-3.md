After packing up, we took some time to stroll the city and see the cute buildings and signs. The streets were empty and the shops closed so it was really peaceful :)

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783277683/20260703_121821_pcfvuk.jpg "Two bookstores in this tiny town...ah the life!")

We stopped by their Starbucks and I decided to try my go to that typically doesn't fair, a hot caramel latte. Here is Norway's take on "Jenna".

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783277795/20260703_122914_u4v9tu.jpg "Love seeing how differently my name is interpreted in other countries! Have yet to fulfill the bucket list item of saying a different name at Starbucks though..")

It was bad. Again. It's time to give up on trying Starbucks lol.

We loaded up and headed on one of the most beautiful road trips we have ever taken! It was about 3.5 hours to our next stay.

Guess how much food was available on the way! None. Jenna went without lunch today until four pm. But the restaurant was in the middle of nowhere! We followed a winding, gravel road for several kilometers, wondering if this location was real... or more serious...open!

Our booked stay for the evening was on a farm! Its smack dab in the middle of a valley in Lysebotn and could not be more breathtaking or picturesque!

{% gallery_block %7B%22photos%22%3A%5B%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783278569%2F20260703_180447_ysljsg.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783278564%2F20260703_181749_c4nrn0.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783278558%2F20260703_180431_p0jkck.jpg%22%2C%22https%3A%2F%2Fres.cloudinary.com%2Fdgw35sldo%2Fimage%2Fupload%2Fc_limit%2Cf_auto%2Cq_auto%2Cw_1200%2Fv1783278552%2F20260703_182400_el3ag1.jpg%22%5D%2C%22caption%22%3A%22The%20barn%20is%20an%20open%20spot%20with%20couches%20and%20chairs%20covered%20in%20a%20sheep%20skins!%22%7D %}

We were greeted by the kindest Spanish  young woman who seemed genuinely happy to have us! She walked us around the property, showed us our rooms, and we were instructed to wear these between buildings. She also explained the BnB has been run by the same family for six generations! The staff lives and works on site!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783279342/20260703_180525_fzdfil.jpg "I never saw myself ever wearing crocs... they aren't uncomfortable.")

They don't have food on site so we hurried to the one restaurant in town she recommended (the other establishment in town is a pub). They had a group coming in at 7 (30 minutes from now) so they said it was good timing for us to be served - super small establishment attached to a hostel!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1783279826/20260703_185510_anehhr.jpg "The fish was crumbly??")

We ordered the lapskaus, a traditional Norwegian stew of diced potato, carrot, and leek in a light broth and the fish & chips. Both were so bad. But we couldn't really expect a lot from this spot. 

Back at our BnB we cozied up by the fireplace and read books! We were allowed to build a fire so Mike got a roaring one going to keep us warm :) Such a relaxing night!
