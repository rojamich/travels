We were over the moon for our next flight - our first lie-flat experience!!

They look like miniature offices with extended chairs, pillows, mattress toppers, blankets, and hygiene kits - the lotion smells divine!

Our stewardess, Chang, was very kind and patient! She approached me right off and we had this hilarious exchange:

Apprehensively approaches me, Chang: Have you selected your menu for the evening.?

Me: Yes, thank you!

Chang: ...are you sure? 

Me: yes :) thank you!

Chang: Have you had congee before?

Me (now more nervous): no, but I'm trying to try something new and be adventurous, thank you!

Chang (unsure but not wanting to argue).

  


We were served dinner (at 1am our time, which was odd but if you know me, I love food so here we are).

They just kept feeding us!! I was so full. Although I wasn't entirely sure what I was trying, there was definitely seafood in there. 

Our menu selection said:

Creamy asparagus/ pea soup (best part)

Fresh salad with Vinaigrette dressing. 

Bread/garlic bread

Smoked duck breast salad

Stir-fried abalone and chicken with fried rice and seasonal veggies.

Seasonal fresh fruits, cheeses, and chocolate cake with raspberry sauce. 

_Facts_ : I tried just about everything - veggies did not look like my jam.

I now _know_ that I enjoy cranberries, raspberries, and walnuts.

_Reconfirmed_ my love of bread and distaste for most seafood.

I SO WISH I would've checked what this brown powder was before dumping it in my congee (essentially a grits or porrage) but the powder is fish. VERY strong-smelling fish. 

Chang once again rushed over.

Chang: Have you had this before? (Motioning to the egg)

Me (pointing at the egg): Yes!

Chang (motioning towards the egg and saying something I can't hear).

Me: hard-boiled egg?

Chang: Yes! Salt egg!

Me: oh yes, I love hard-boiled eggs!

Chang looked pleased and I was encouraged.

  


Now, I understand. When she said salt egg, it did not even taste like egg. Just salt. OH MY. I alternated between a bite of fish congee, salt egg, steamed bun, and blackberries. 

Our menu selection said:

Plain congee, stir-fried mushrooms and veggies, shrimp & asparagus, cucumber, fish floss/seaweed, salty egg, steamed bun, fresh fruits. 

Facts: I tried just about everything - veggies did not look like my jam.

I now _know_  I do not enjoy salted eggs, blackberries by themselves, or congee.

I have _reconfirmed_ my hatred of fish.   
  


I was able to do some stretching in the lie flat and then we sat back and relaxed for the final hour of our flight while reading and sipping green tea :)

Truthfully, it was the first flight I didn't want to end!
