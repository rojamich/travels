Today was a relatively early wake-up for the Rojas family to prepare for our hike! So stoked to explore more of Svalbard. If you've read [The Midnight Library](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/52578297-the-midnight-library) by Matt Haig, you will understand some of my excitement!

Our guide met us promptly and we learned he is originally from Mexico City (which we love!) our other group member is from Germany (also love)! We never fail to go on a tour with a German during our travels. Many Germans are very well-traveled, often taking a gap year before college. This is likely due to their 4-6 weeks paid time off per year plus holiday leave! Many Germans are also bilingual/multilingual. I just looked it up and approximately 67% of Germans are multilingual. How incredible is that!? Only about a quarter of Americans are bilingual!

Our small group was taken to the office to be fitted for rubber boots to avoid getting too wet on the hike. Each time we are fitted for shoes, we have to remember our EU size - not the US size! Look how cute their coat hangers are!

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dgw35sldo/image/upload/c_limit,f_auto,q_auto,w_1200/v1782408588/20260625_081016_jhv05g.jpg)

It was a short drive to the start of the hike where we were given our MRE (Meal Ready to Eat) and a large thermos with hot water for them later. Our German companion announced he had brought no water and routinely asked the guide for some. He also did not bring a backpack so he couldn't carry any equipment or his food. 

Our hike began near an old coal mining factory.
