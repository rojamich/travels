Some things we wish we had done/known for London.

Two more days would have been ideal for London. Less rushing around and a more relaxed atmosphere would have helped us be more immersed in the vacation and absorb London better. A whole day trip to Stonehenge is really necessary if you want to grasp the entire journey the creators likely went through. I think I would have preferred to drive out there ourselves and also stay a night in Salisbury! That town was just dang cute!

The driving in London is more hectic than most of the US. The cars/buses don’t seem to care if people or bicyclists are in their way and vice versa. Also, people rarely look where they’re going when they walk or they walk three people across the sidewalks so you have to move out of their way! We watched a man scream at a woman for being in his walking path and shoulder-check her in to next Tuesday!

The COVID restrictions were fairly relaxed and no masks were mandated. You had to be fully vaccinated or have a negative COVID test to fly. Our only requirements for arriving in London was a passenger locator form for each of us and a negative COVID test on day two, which no one followed-up on.

Michael got a Labara SIM card for his phone to operate in London more efficiently. While his was cheaper, we much preferred my EE SIM card here. 

City Mapper was a life-saving app that allowed us to navigate our way around the city more efficiently. It gives you multiple ways to travel and includes costs and time to travel for each. Google maps is not the most ideal navigation app here but will still work in offline mode with the locations downloaded.

The bus was the most convenient form of travel here and is nothing like the buses in the US. The riders are just normal, daily commuters and it makes for a relaxed way of getting around the city. The metro was moderately easy to use but felt rushed and crowded. 

The water in the restaurants costs to drink, it does not come with your meal for free. The bathrooms are freely accessible though, not for charge. 

The diversity of languages there was surprising! We thought most stores would have English speaking employees but ran into quite a few people speaking only Panjabi or Urdu. 

In London, it took forever to remember everyone drives on the left side of the road! So, you also need to remember to go up and down stairs on the left side and walk on the left side of the sidewalks. 

Days visited: February 7th\- 11th

Daily weather: High 40s in the morning and evenings, mid 50s in the middle of the day.

Total distance walked: 42.2 miles

Transportations used: metro, buses, train, uber, and walking.
