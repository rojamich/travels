It was finally time to visit the prison where Mandela was held. We were led by an actual ex-political prisoner of the establishment who talked about what life was like and his experience being held here and oppressed during this time. At times here in Cape Town overall, Mike and I felt like we were moving back in time to see what the US had been like, shortly after segregation and watching how the country grew and changed after such massive cultural overhauls.

We learned about the separate meals offered to prisoners depending on race, their difference in treatment, and even in uniforms. Visitors were granted twice a year, were limited time, no physical touch, and the children must be over age 16 to visit. The age of adulthood is 18 here.
